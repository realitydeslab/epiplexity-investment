# DELL — Leader Profile 👤
*Dell Technologies | Updated: 2026-03-07*

## Michael Dell — Founder & CEO

### Track Record
- Founded Dell in 1984 (age 19), took it private in $24.9B LBO (2013), re-listed via VMware tracking stock (2018)
- Successfully navigated the PC commoditization era, EMC acquisition ($67B, 2016), VMware spin-off
- Now executing the AI pivot — arguably the most significant strategic shift since the EMC deal

### Leadership Assessment
- **Execution:** Strong. Dell has beaten earnings estimates 4 consecutive quarters. FY2026 revenue of $113.5B up 19% YoY. AI server revenue grew 342% YoY in Q4.
- **Vision:** Positioned Dell as the "AI Factory" — full-stack infrastructure from edge to data center. Joint roadmap with NVIDIA.
- **Capital allocation:** Disciplined. $7.5B returned to shareholders in FY2026 while maintaining growth investment. 20% dividend increase signals confidence.
- **Concern:** Michael Dell sold $2.2B in shares during 2025 (June and October). Did NOT use a 10b5-1 pre-arranged plan, which is unusual for CEO-level sales. However, he still owns ~47% of the company (~$50B+ worth) — selling 4% of holdings is not alarming.

### Insider Activity
- **Michael Dell:** Sold 16.2M shares worth $2.2B in 2025. Large absolute number but small relative to holdings.
- **No 10b5-1 plan** — discretionary selling raises some eyebrows but could be for philanthropy ($6.25B donation announced with wife)
- **David Kennedy** named permanent CFO (from interim) in Q3 FY2026 — positive signal of organizational stability

### Key Hires/Departures
- **David Kennedy → permanent CFO** (Nov 2025): Was interim, now permanent. Provides stability.
- **Jeff Clarke (COO):** Continues to lead the AI server strategy. His commentary on "$30B AI orders YTD" and pipeline being "multiples of backlog" is the most bullish signal from any Dell executive.

### Verdict
Michael Dell is the right leader for this transition. He's navigated bigger pivots (going private, EMC, VMware). The insider selling is a yellow flag but not a red one given philanthropy context and massive remaining stake. The "One Dell Way" operational restructuring shows he's not resting on AI momentum.

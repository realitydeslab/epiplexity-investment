# META — Structured Intelligence 🧠

*Research Date: March 6, 2026*

## What Does This Tell Us About the Sector?

### The AI Ad Flywheel Is Real
Meta's 22% revenue growth at $200B scale proves that AI-driven ad targeting is not hype — it's delivering measurable ROI for advertisers. This validates the entire digital ad stack: better AI → better targeting → higher CPMs → more ad spend. Every company in the ad ecosystem benefits.

### Open-Source AI Is a Viable Business Strategy
Meta's Llama strategy (give away the model, monetize the applications) mirrors Red Hat/Linux but at hyperscale. This means:
- Proprietary AI model companies (OpenAI) face pricing pressure
- Cloud providers (AWS, Azure, GCP) need to compete on inference cost
- Open-source creates an ecosystem moat that's harder to replicate than a proprietary model

### Big Tech AI Capex Is Sustainable
Meta spent ~$40B+ on capex in 2025 and still generated $46B FCF. The market worried about "AI overspend" — the data shows these companies can fund massive AI investment without compromising returns. This is bullish for NVDA, AMD, AVGO, and the entire AI infrastructure stack.

## Contrarian Angle — What's the Market Missing?

### 1. Meta AI as a Search Engine
The market still values Meta as a social media / advertising company. But Meta AI with 3.3B+ distribution could become the world's most-used AI assistant — and eventually a search alternative. If Meta captures even 5% of Google's search ad revenue, that's $10B+ incremental.

### 2. WhatsApp Is Massively Undermonetized
WhatsApp has 2B+ users and generates maybe $2-3B in revenue. WeChat (Tencent) generates $10B+ from a similar user base in China alone. The gap between WhatsApp's current monetization and its potential is enormous.

### 3. The Tax Rate Anomaly Masks True Earnings Power
2025 EPS declined 1.6% despite 22% revenue growth because the effective tax rate jumped from 11.8% to 29.6%. The market is anchoring on flat EPS. If tax rates normalize to 20-25%, true earnings power is $26-28 EPS for 2025, not $23.49.

### 4. Capex Cycle Will Peak
AI infrastructure capex ($60-65B in 2026) will eventually slow as data centers are built out. When capex intensity declines and revenue continues growing, FCF will explode. The market is pricing current FCF margins, not future ones.

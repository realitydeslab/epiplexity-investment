# PLTR Competitive Map — Palantir Technologies

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

## Direct Competitors

### Enterprise AI Platforms
| Company | Ticker | Overlap | Threat Level |
|---------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Snowflake | SNOW | Data cloud, enterprise AI | Medium — different layer (data vs. operations) |
| Databricks | Private | Data + AI platform | Medium-High — closest competitor in enterprise |
| C3.ai | AI | Enterprise AI platform | Low — much smaller, struggling execution |
| Salesforce | CRM | Agentforce enterprise AI | Medium — different buyer (CRM vs. ops) |

### Government/Defense AI
| Company | Ticker | Overlap | Threat Level |
|---------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Anduril | Private | Defense AI, autonomous systems | Medium — hardware focus vs. Palantir's software |
| Booz Allen | BAH | Defense consulting + AI | Low-Medium — consulting vs. platform |
| SAIC | SAIC | Government IT + AI | Low — legacy integrator |

## Who Benefits from the Same Catalysts?

- **Government AI spending increase:** Booz Allen (BAH), Leidos (LDOS), SAIC
- **Enterprise AI adoption:** Microsoft (MSFT), Snowflake (SNOW), Databricks
- **Defense rearmament:** LMT, RTX, NOC, Anduril
- **AI infrastructure demand:** NVDA, AVGO, MSFT Azure

## Who Loses if Palantir Wins?

- **Legacy defense IT integrators:** SAIC, Leidos — Palantir displaces traditional contract models
- **Consulting firms:** Accenture, Deloitte — AIP reduces need for large consulting deployments
- **Point solution vendors:** Various niche analytics firms that Palantir's platform subsumes

## Pair Trade Opportunities

1. **Long PLTR / Short AI (C3.ai):** Both target enterprise AI but Palantir executing, C3 struggling
2. **Long PLTR / Short legacy defense IT (SAIC):** Palantir's platform model disrupts traditional integrators
3. **Long PLTR + SNOW basket:** Both benefit from enterprise data-to-AI trend, complementary layers

## Palantir's Moat

1. **Data integration complexity:** Years of work connecting siloed government/enterprise data — extremely hard to replicate
2. **Security clearances:** Staff with TS/SCI clearances; takes years to build
3. **AIP network effects:** Each boot camp creates advocates; 180 deals >$1M shows breadth
4. **Switching costs:** Once Palantir is the operational backbone, ripping it out is nearly impossible
5. **Regulatory moat:** FedRAMP, IL5/IL6 certifications create barriers

## Key Insight

Palantir has no true peer. It's the only company that spans defense intelligence + enterprise AI operations + government data analytics at this scale with this growth rate. The closest "competitor" is probably Microsoft (Azure + Copilot) but they operate at a different layer and are actually a partner in many cases.

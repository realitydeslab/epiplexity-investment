# PLTR Investment Thesis — Palantir Technologies

*Research date: March 6, 2026 | Price: $157.14*

## The Catalyst

Palantir is executing the fastest enterprise AI commercialization in history. US commercial revenue grew 137% YoY in Q4 2025 and is guided for 115%+ in 2026. The AIP platform has created a new category — the "AI operating system" — that converts boot camp participants into production customers at an unprecedented rate. Each quarterly earnings will likely bring guidance raises, just as FY2025 started at $2.65B guidance and delivered $4.475B.

The next 12 months bring: Q1 earnings (~May), DHS $1B BPA task orders, UK MoD contract activation (April), potential NATO expansion, and continued DOD wins. FY2026 revenue guide of $7.19B (+61% YoY) is likely conservative given the pattern of massive beats.

## The Trade

- **Entry:** $157 (current), or $135-140 on any market dip — DCA recommended
- **Base target:** $200 (+27%) — meets guidance, stock re-approaches ATH
- **Bull target:** $250 (+59%) — beats to $8B+, multiple sustains
- **Stop-loss:** $130 (-17%)
- **Position size:** 3-5% of portfolio

## The Risk

**Valuation is the entire risk.** At 52x FY2026 revenue, Palantir is priced for perfection. The fundamentals are exceptional — 70% growth, 41% GAAP operating margins, $4B+ FCF, $7.2B cash — but the market has front-loaded years of future value. Insider selling of $400M in two weeks (including Peter Thiel's $280M dump) is a yellow flag. Any growth deceleration, even to a still-excellent 40%, could trigger a 30-40% correction.

Secondary risks: government spending cuts (DOGE could cut both ways), political controversy around ICE/surveillance work, SBC dilution gap between GAAP and adjusted metrics.

## The Verdict

**CAUTIOUS BUY — Exceptional business at a rich price.**

Palantir is the most impressive AI business in the public markets today. Rule of 40 at 127%, accelerating growth across all segments, expanding margins, and a $4B+ FCF machine. The AIP platform has genuine moat characteristics — data integration complexity, security clearances, and switching costs.

But at $375B market cap on $7.2B guided revenue, you need sustained 50%+ growth for years to justify the price. The insiders are selling aggressively. The business is "n of 1" as Karp says — but so is the valuation.

**Best approach:** Start a position on any 10%+ pullback from here. Size modestly (3-5%). Let quarterly beats justify adding. This is a stock where being right on the business doesn't guarantee being right on the trade if you overpay.

## The Catalyst

**MI450 launches H2 2026** — AMD's most advanced AI GPU (2nm, 40 PFLOPS FP4, 432GB HBM4) arrives to challenge NVIDIA's Vera Rubin. Backed by a $50B+ design win backlog and 6GW capacity commitments from OpenAI and Meta, this is AMD's defining moment in AI compute.

## The Trade

AMD is trading at ~$216, down 25% from its January $275 high after Q4 2025 earnings "disappointed" on aggressive (not consensus) expectations. Revenue actually grew 34% to $10.3B and data center hit $5.4B (+39%). The pullback creates an entry point ahead of the most catalyst-rich 12 months in AMD's history.

- **Forward P/E:** ~29-33x on 2027E EPS of $11 — cheap for a 35% grower
- **PEG ratio:** -55% below sector median (Motley Fool)
- **Beat history:** 8 of 10 last quarters

## The Scenarios ($10K → 12 Months)

- 🚀 **Bull ($330):** MI450 on time, 60% DC CAGR, OpenAI/Meta revenue ramps → **$15,278 (+53%)**
- 📊 **Base ($270):** Solid execution, 35% revenue growth → **$12,500 (+25%)**
- 🐻 **Bear ($165):** MI450 delayed, NVIDIA dominates, custom ASICs accelerate → **$7,639 (-24%)**

**Expected value:** Skewed bullish. Risk/reward is ~2:1 at current price.

## The Risk

1. **CoWoS packaging bottleneck** — TSMC capacity limits MI450 shipments
2. **NVIDIA's ecosystem moat** — CUDA is 20 years of developer lock-in
3. **Custom ASICs** — Google, Amazon, Meta building their own chips
4. **Execution risk** — 35% CAGR target is ambitious; any stumble gets punished at these multiples

## The Verdict

**BUY on pullbacks to $200-216.** AMD is the highest-conviction #2 play in AI compute. The MI450 launch, OpenAI/Meta backlog, and inference cost advantage create a catalyst-dense next 12 months. The 25% pullback from highs is an entry gift — the fundamental story has not changed.

**Position:** 5-8% of portfolio. Stop-loss at $180. Scale in.

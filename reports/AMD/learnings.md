# AMD — Structured Intelligence 🧠

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

---

## What This Tells Us About the Sector

1. **The AI compute market is big enough for #2.** AMD's $50B+ backlog proves hyperscalers WANT a NVIDIA alternative. Diversification of supply is a strategic imperative for Meta, OpenAI, Microsoft.

2. **Inference is the next battleground.** Training requires CUDA ecosystem lock-in; inference is more commoditized. AMD's 30% tokens-per-dollar advantage on MI355X matters because inference scales with users, not training runs.

3. **The CUDA moat is eroding, slowly.** ROCm + ZLUDA + native framework support means the switching cost is declining. Not gone — but declining. This is a 2-3 year trend, not overnight.

4. **CoWoS packaging is THE bottleneck.** Both AMD and NVIDIA are constrained by TSMC's advanced packaging capacity. Whoever secures more CoWoS allocation wins.

## Adjacent Plays

- **TSM (TSMC):** Manufactures everything. Agnostic winner regardless of AMD vs NVIDIA outcome.
- **ANET (Arista):** AI networking scales with GPU deployments from either vendor.
- **VRT (Vertiv):** Power infrastructure is GPU-agnostic.
- **MRVL (Marvell):** Custom silicon + networking — complementary to AMD's merchant GPU approach.

## Contrarian Angle — What's the Market Missing?

**The market may be underpricing AMD's inference moat.** Everyone focuses on training (where NVIDIA dominates), but inference is 60-70% of AI compute spend and growing. AMD's MI355X already wins on cost-per-token. MI450 extends this lead. If inference becomes the primary revenue driver for AI chips (which it will as models are deployed at scale), AMD's TAM expands faster than consensus models.

**The 42% valuation gap (Motley Fool, Mar 5, 2026):** AMD trades at forward PEG -55% below sector median and -45% below its 5-year average. For a company growing revenue 35% with clear catalysts, this discount is anomalous.

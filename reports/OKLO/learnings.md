## What This Tells Us About the Sector

1. **Nuclear renaissance is real but SLOW.** AI data center demand is creating genuine pull for nuclear baseload power, but regulatory timelines remain the bottleneck. The market keeps overestimating speed.

2. **Pre-revenue nuclear companies trade on narrative, not fundamentals.** OKLO at $14-15B market cap with zero revenue is priced on pure optionality. The sector is in a speculative bubble driven by the AI power demand story.

3. **DOE is the new accelerator.** The Reactor Pilot Program under the Trump administration is creating a parallel, faster pathway to deployment. This is a genuine structural change in how nuclear gets built in the US.

4. **HALEU is the chokepoint.** Every advanced reactor needs it, almost nobody can supply it commercially. LEU (Centrus) is the picks-and-shovels play here.

5. **Insiders are selling.** Across nuclear names, insider sales are elevated. The people closest to these companies are taking money off the table while retail piles in.

## Contrarian Angle — What's the Market Missing?

### Bull Contrarian
The market is **overweighting NRC risk** and **underweighting DOE pathway.** The DOE Reactor Pilot Program is a structural change — it allows DOE-authorized sites to operate while NRC licensing proceeds in parallel. If Aurora-INL achieves criticality under DOE authority in 2026, the stock re-rates massively before NRC even finishes reviewing the COLA.

### Bear Contrarian
The market is **underweighting HALEU risk.** Management's own admission that commercial HALEU may not be available until the 2030s means even if the reactor works, it may not have fuel to run at commercial scale. The 14 GW pipeline is meaningless without fuel.

### The Real Question
Is Oklo a **technology company** (fast reactor design + fuel recycling IP) or a **utility** (power generation)? The market prices it as both, but it needs to become a utility to generate revenue, and utility multiples are 10-15x earnings, not infinite (pre-revenue). If Oklo succeeds, the terminal value is a utility with premium IP — maybe $20-30B. At $14-15B today, you're paying close to terminal value with 5+ years of execution risk.

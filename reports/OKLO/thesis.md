# OKLO — Investment Thesis (1-Page)

**Ticker:** OKLO (NYSE) | **Price:** $58.29 | **Market Cap:** ~$14-15B | **Date:** 2026-03-07

---

## The Catalyst

**Aurora-INL criticality — mid-2026.** Oklo's first commercial reactor at Idaho National Laboratory is under construction. If it achieves first criticality (self-sustaining nuclear reaction) by mid-2026, it becomes the first advanced fast reactor to operate in the US in decades. This single event would validate the technology, de-risk the 14+ GW customer pipeline, and potentially double the stock.

Secondary catalyst: Fuel recycling. Oklo's NRC-approved fuel recycling facility (Jan 2026) could create a vertically integrated moat — build reactors AND supply their own fuel from used nuclear waste. No competitor has this.

## The Trade

**Wait, don't chase.** At $58, you're paying $14-15B for a pre-revenue company with $0 revenue, accelerating losses ($36M/quarter), and a CEO who sold $130M in shares near the top. The bull case requires perfect execution on nuclear timelines (historically unreliable).

- **Best entry:** $45-50 (better risk/reward) or post-criticality confirmation at $80-90 (pay up for de-risked thesis)
- **Position size:** 2-3% max — this is a speculation, not an investment
- **Stop-loss:** $45 hard stop

## The Risk

1. **HALEU fuel supply** — management admits commercial quantities may not be available until 2030s
2. **Timeline slippage** — first commercial power already pushed from 2026 to late 2027-2028; bearish NRC meeting notes suggest further delays possible
3. **Insider selling** — CEO sold $130M+ in 6 weeks (Dec 2024-Feb 2025). Loudest signal in the data.
4. **Valuation** — $14-15B for $0 revenue. Even success delivers a utility worth $20-30B. Risk/reward is thin at current price.
5. **Dilution** — $540M ATM already done; potential for more capital raises

## The Verdict

**HOLD / WAIT FOR BETTER ENTRY.** ⏳

Oklo has the most compelling advanced nuclear story: proven EBR-II heritage, massive customer pipeline, $1.2B cash fortress, DOE backing, Sam Altman halo, and unique fuel recycling capability. But the stock is priced for success that's 2-5 years away, the CEO is selling, and nuclear timelines always slip.

**The trade:** Build a watchlist position. Buy aggressively at $45-50 or on Aurora criticality confirmation. Don't chase the narrative at $58.

**Better risk-adjusted nuclear plays:** LEU (HALEU monopoly, $2.3B backlog), CEG (operating nuclear fleet), CCJ (uranium supply).

**Epiplexity Score: 68/100** — Compelling technology, dangerous valuation, execution risk too high at current price.

# ARM Holdings (ARM) — Investment Thesis

**Date:** March 6, 2026 | **Price:** ~$121 | **Target:** $150 (base) / $180 (bull)

---

## The One-Liner

ARM is the architectural toll booth of the AI era — collecting royalties on every AI chip shipped, from cloud GPUs to edge devices to autonomous vehicles, with pricing power accelerating through Armv9 and CSS adoption.

## The Catalyst

**Armv9 + CSS = structural royalty rate uplift.** This isn't about selling more chips — it's about charging 2-3x more per chip. Data center royalties grew 100%+ YoY last quarter. Hyperscalers (AWS, Google, Microsoft) are all building custom Arm silicon and paying premium CSS rates. This is a multi-year revenue upgrade cycle that's still in early innings.

**Next trigger:** Q4 FY2026 earnings (May 6) — guided $0.58 EPS. A beat here, combined with strong FY2027 guidance, re-rates the stock toward $150+.

## The Trade

- **Entry:** $121 (current consolidation base)
- **Base target:** $150 (+24%, 12 months)
- **Bull target:** $180 (+49%, if catalysts accelerate)
- **Stop-loss:** $100 (-17%)
- **Position size:** 3-5% of portfolio

## The Risk

1. **Valuation is demanding** — trading ~60x forward P/E. Any execution stumble gets punished hard.
2. **Licensing revenue is lumpy** — one bad quarter can crater the stock 15-20%.
3. **SoftBank overhang** — majority owner could sell at any time.
4. **RISC-V** — long-term threat, but not a 12-month concern.

## The Verdict

**BUY on the consolidation.** ARM at $121 is 25% below its ATH and trading at 62% discount to the $203 valuation model target. The growth story is intact and accelerating in the highest-value segments (data center, automotive). The risk/reward from this level is compelling — you're paying for 25% growth and getting free optionality on edge AI, automotive, and PC market share gains.

The key question isn't whether ARM is a great business (it is — 97% gross margins, architectural monopoly). The question is whether $121 prices in enough of the upside. With data center royalties doubling and Armv9 mix still shifting, we think no.

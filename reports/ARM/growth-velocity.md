# ARM Holdings (ARM) — Growth Velocity 📈

**Date:** March 6, 2026

---

## Revenue Growth (Last 4 Quarters)

| Quarter | Revenue | YoY Growth | QoQ Growth |
|---------|---------|------------|------------|
| Q4 FY2025 (Mar 2025) | ~$1.05B | ~47% | — |
| Q1 FY2026 (Jun 2025) | ~$1.1B | ~30% | +5% |
| Q2 FY2026 (Sep 2025) | ~$1.15B | ~28% | +5% |
| Q3 FY2026 (Dec 2025) | $1.24B | 26% | +8% |

**Verdict:** YoY growth decelerating from the 47% peak (tough comps) but stabilizing in the mid-20s range. QoQ growth actually accelerated in Q3. **Growth is healthy, not decelerating dangerously.**

## Revenue Breakdown

- **Royalty Revenue (Q3):** $737M (+27% YoY) — record quarter
- **Licensing Revenue (Q3):** $505M (+25% YoY) — strong but lumpy
- **TTM Revenue:** ~$4.7B (+24% YoY)

## Key Growth Drivers

### Data Center (Accelerating) 🔥
- Data center royalties grew **100%+ YoY** in Q3
- Hyperscalers (AWS Graviton, Google Axion, Microsoft Cobalt) all ramping on Arm
- CSS adoption creating structural royalty rate uplift

### Armv9 Mix Shift (Accelerating) 🔥
- Now >50% of royalty revenue
- 2-3x royalty rate vs. v8
- Every quarter, mix shifts further toward v9

### Automotive (Accelerating) 🔥
- Growing 40%+ YoY
- New design wins (Rivian production vehicle on custom Arm silicon)
- Software-Defined Vehicle trend = more Arm cores per car

### Smartphone (Stable/Decelerating)
- Still largest single end market
- CFO quantified worst case: 20% unit decline = only 2% royalty hit (v9 mix offsets)
- Not a growth driver, but not a drag either

## Guidance vs. Actual History

- ARM has **beat consensus EPS** in each of the last 4 quarters
- Q3 FY2026: EPS $0.43 vs. $0.41 est. (+5% beat)
- Management guided Q4 midpoint EPS $0.58 — in line but conservative historically
- **Pattern: guide conservatively, beat modestly.** Predictable.

## Unit Economics

- **Gross margin:** ~97% — pure IP licensing model, minimal COGS
- **Operating margin:** ~41% (Q3), down from 45% YoY due to investment spending
- **R&D investment:** Increasing to support CSS and new verticals
- Free cash flow declined last quarter due to capex and working capital — monitor this

## Path to Profitability

Already highly profitable. Question is operating leverage:
- Revenue growing 25%+ while opex grows slower
- As CSS adoption scales, royalty revenue becomes more predictable
- FY2027 consensus: $5.7-6B revenue, operating margins expanding back toward 45%+

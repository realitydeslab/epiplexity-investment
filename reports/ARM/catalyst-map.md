# ARM Holdings (ARM) — Catalyst Map 🎯

**Date:** March 6, 2026 | **Current Price:** ~$121 | **52-Week Range:** $63–$160

---

## 1. Q4 FY2026 Earnings (May 6, 2026)

- **What:** Final quarter of FY2026. Guided midpoint EPS of $0.58 — significant step-up from Q3's $0.43. Revenue expected ~$1.3B+.
- **Impact:** +10-15% on beat (stock surged 6% on Q3 beat). Miss could send it to $100.
- **Probability:** High (management has beat 4 consecutive quarters)
- **Market expectation:** Modest beat baked in. Licensing lumpiness is the wild card.
- **Our edge:** Data center royalties grew 100%+ YoY in Q3 — this trend is accelerating, not decelerating. CSS adoption is still early innings.

## 2. Armv9 Royalty Rate Inflection (Ongoing through 2026)

- **What:** Armv9 now >50% of royalty revenue (up from ~30% a year ago). Each v9 chip carries 2-3x the royalty rate of v8.
- **When:** Every quarter — mix shift is structural and accelerating.
- **Impact:** +15-20% annual royalty growth even on flat unit volumes.
- **Probability:** High — already happening, just not fully priced.
- **Our edge:** Market still partially values ARM as a smartphone volume play. The per-chip value increase from v9 is a pricing power story.

## 3. Hyperscaler Custom Chip Wins (H1-H2 2026)

- **What:** Amazon Graviton 5, Google Axion next-gen, Microsoft Cobalt 200 — all built on Arm CSS. New design wins from additional hyperscalers possible.
- **When:** Product announcements throughout 2026.
- **Impact:** Each new CSS customer adds $50-100M+ in annual royalty revenue at mature volumes. +5-8% per major win.
- **Probability:** High — pipeline already signed. Question is timing of volume ramps.
- **Our edge:** CSS is a 2-3x royalty multiplier vs. legacy licenses. Market underestimates revenue impact.

## 4. Automotive ADAS/SDV Design Wins (2026)

- **What:** Arm winning "Software-Defined Vehicle" architecture. Rivian announced first production vehicle on custom Arm silicon. More OEMs expected.
- **When:** Design win announcements H1-H2 2026, revenue contribution 2027-2028.
- **Impact:** Automotive <10% of revenue but growing 40%+ YoY. Not priced in.
- **Probability:** Medium-High.
- **Our edge:** Street models underweight automotive revenue contribution.

## 5. AI Inference at the Edge (2026)

- **What:** Arm's AI extensions (SVE2) enable on-device AI inference without dedicated accelerators. As AI moves cloud→edge, Arm captures more value.
- **When:** Throughout 2026.
- **Impact:** +5-10% incremental royalty growth from AI-capable edge devices.
- **Probability:** Medium.
- **Market expectation:** Priced for cloud AI, not edge AI. Edge is optionality.

## 6. PC Market Share Gains (2026)

- **What:** Arm-based Windows PCs (Qualcomm Snapdragon X Elite, others) gaining traction.
- **When:** OEM launches throughout 2026.
- **Impact:** PC is new TAM — even 10% share is meaningful. +3-5%.
- **Probability:** Medium.

## 7. Index Rebalancing / Weight Increase

- **What:** As free float increases (SoftBank lockup expirations), index weight could increase, driving passive buying.
- **When:** Potential mid-2026.
- **Impact:** +3-5% from passive flows.
- **Probability:** Medium.

## 8. Potential Licensing Mega-Deal

- **What:** New major licensing agreement. Licensing revenue is lumpy — one deal can make a quarter.
- **When:** Any time.
- **Impact:** +5-10% on announcement.
- **Probability:** Medium.

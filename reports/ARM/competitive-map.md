# ARM Holdings (ARM) — Competitive Map 🗺️

**Date:** March 6, 2026

---

## Who Benefits from the SAME Catalysts?

### AI Data Center Build-Out
- **NVDA** — GPU compute, Arm's biggest customer (Grace CPU uses Arm)
- **AVGO** — Custom ASICs for hyperscalers (competing for same design budgets but complementary)
- **TSM** — Manufactures all Arm-based chips. Pure picks-and-shovels beneficiary.
- **MRVL** — Custom silicon + networking. Direct Arm CSS customer.

### Automotive/SDV
- **Qualcomm (QCOM)** — Snapdragon Ride/Digital Chassis, major Arm licensee
- **NXP (NXPI)** — Auto chips, Arm-based
- **Infineon (IFNNY)** — Power + MCU for EVs

### Edge AI
- **QCOM** — Snapdragon X Elite for PCs, Arm-based
- **AAPL** — Apple Silicon is Arm-based; validates the architecture for premium compute

## Who Loses if ARM Wins?

### Direct Losers
- **Intel (INTC)** — Every Arm server chip displaces an x86 Xeon. Intel is losing data center share to Arm-based custom silicon. This is a zero-sum fight.
- **AMD (AMD)** — To a lesser extent. AMD's EPYC competes with Arm in cloud, but AMD also licenses Arm for some designs. Mixed relationship.

### Indirect Losers
- **RISC-V ecosystem** — If Arm CSS locks in hyperscalers with pre-integrated designs, RISC-V's window to compete in high-performance compute narrows.

## Pair Trade Opportunities

### Long ARM / Short INTC
- **Thesis:** Structural shift from x86 to Arm in data center. Intel's foundry struggles + Arm's CSS momentum = diverging trajectories.
- **Risk:** Intel turnaround under new leadership, government subsidies (CHIPS Act).

### Long ARM + Long TSM (Basket)
- **Thesis:** TSM manufactures every Arm chip. Both benefit from Arm architecture proliferation. TSM gives you the volume angle, ARM gives you the IP/pricing angle.

### Long ARM / Short Generic Semis (SOXX)
- **Thesis:** ARM is a structural winner within semis. If the sector sells off, ARM should outperform on relative basis due to margin resilience.

## Competitive Moat Assessment

| Moat Factor | Strength | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Architecture lock-in | Very Strong | 99% of smartphones, growing in servers. Switching costs enormous. |
| Software ecosystem | Strong | Decades of compiler, OS, and application optimization. RISC-V can't replicate this quickly. |
| CSS pre-integration | Growing | Reduces time-to-market for customers. Creates dependency. |
| RISC-V threat | Low-Medium | Credible in low-end IoT. Years away from competitive in data center. |
| Customer concentration | Risk | SoftBank majority owner. Top 5 licensees = large % of revenue. |

# ASML — Leader Profile 👤

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

## CEO: Christophe Fouquet

- **Role:** President & CEO since April 2024 (succeeded Peter Wennink)
- **Background:** 16+ years at ASML. Previously EVP/CTO and head of EUV business. Deep technical roots — he built the EUV program.
- **Reputation:** Continuity hire. Not a disruptor but the architect of the High-NA transition. Knows every customer by name.
- **Key strength:** Technical credibility with TSMC, Intel, Samsung. These are relationship-driven, multi-billion-dollar equipment decisions.
- **Risk:** First full year as CEO during a tricky transition (China headwind + High-NA ramp). Execution test.

## Public Statements (Recent)

- **Jan 28, 2026 earnings call:** "AI demand is accelerating" — explicitly bullish on 2026 capex cycle. Raised guidance to €34-39B.
- Emphasized that Q4 bookings (€13.2B) reflect real customer commitments, not speculative orders.
- Called High-NA volume shipments "a major milestone for the industry."

## Insider Activity

- **Share buyback:** Company-level buyback at ~€25M/day (€125M/week). This is the strongest insider signal — management deploying capital at ~€1,240-1,290/share.
- **Individual insider transactions:** No significant insider selling flagged in recent reports. Key executives holding.
- **Peter Wennink (former CEO):** Retained board advisory role. No major sales reported post-transition.

## Key Hires/Departures

- Fouquet transition from Wennink was smooth — 6-month overlap in 2024.
- No notable C-suite departures in 2025-2026.
- Continued investment in High-NA engineering teams (Veldhoven + Wilton, CT facility).

## Leadership Assessment

**Grade: A-**

Fouquet is the right CEO for this moment — he literally built the EUV machines that generate 80%+ of ASML's value. The risk is that he's an engineer-CEO in a period requiring geopolitical navigation (China, US export controls). But ASML's product monopoly means the CEO mostly needs to execute, not sell. And he's executing.

# ASML — Competitive Map 🗺️

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

## Direct Competitors (Lithography)

### Canon & Nikon
- **Market share in EUV:** 0%. ASML has 100% monopoly on EUV lithography.
- Canon and Nikon compete only in older DUV (Deep Ultraviolet) and i-line lithography.
- **DUV market share:** ASML ~85%, Canon ~10%, Nikon ~5%
- Neither Canon nor Nikon has EUV capability and won't for at least 10+ years.
- **High-NA EUV:** Zero competition. Period.

### Huawei/SMEE (China Domestic)
- Shanghai Micro Electronics Equipment (SMEE) is developing DUV lithography for Chinese market.
- Currently at 90nm capability — roughly 15 years behind ASML's leading edge.
- Not a competitive threat in the investment horizon. Long-term wildcard for DUV market share in China only.

| KLA Corp | KLAC | Inspection/metrology — more complex chips = more KLA tools needed |

## Who Loses if ASML Wins?

- **Canon/Nikon:** Further marginalized in high-value lithography
- **Multi-patterning tool makers:** High-NA reduces multi-patterning steps, meaning fewer etch/deposition passes per layer
- **Chinese chipmakers** relying on DUV workarounds: High-NA widens the gap

## Pair Trade Opportunities

1. **Long ASML / Short Nikon (7731.T):** ASML's High-NA ramp makes Nikon's litho business increasingly irrelevant
2. **Long ASML + Long KLAC:** Both benefit from node complexity. KLAC is the inspection play alongside ASML's litho play.
3. **Long ASML / Long TSM basket:** "Picks and shovels" of AI — both win regardless of which AI company dominates.

## ASML's Moat Rating

| Moat Dimension | Rating | Notes |
|----------------|--------|-------|
| Technology | ★★★★★ | 10-15 year lead, no one can replicate EUV |
| Customer lock-in | ★★★★★ | Sole supplier — customers MUST buy from ASML |
| Network effects | ★★★☆☆ | Ecosystem of suppliers (Zeiss, IMEC) entrenched |
| Switching costs | ★★★★★ | Impossible to switch — no alternative exists |
| Pricing power | ★★★★★ | Can charge $350M per machine, customers pay |

**This is arguably the strongest competitive moat in global technology.**

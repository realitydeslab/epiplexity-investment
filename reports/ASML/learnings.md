# ASML — Structured Intelligence 🧠

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

## What This Tells Us About the Sector

1. **AI capex cycle is real and accelerating.** ASML's record €13.2B Q4 bookings = chipmakers committing billions to next-gen fabs. This isn't speculative — these are binding purchase orders.

2. **The semiconductor capex cycle is front-loaded.** Equipment makers (ASML, AMAT, LRCX, KLAC) see revenue BEFORE the chips are made. ASML is the earliest signal in the AI value chain.

3. **High-NA is a generational upgrade cycle.** Like the transition from DUV to EUV (~2018-2020), the High-NA transition (2026-2030) creates a multi-year revenue uplift. Each machine costs 75% more than standard EUV.

4. **China decoupling creates volatility but not existential risk.** ASML guided the China headwind transparently. The demand from US/EU/Taiwan/Korea fabs more than compensates.

## Adjacent Plays

- **KLA Corp (KLAC):** More complex chips = more inspection. Correlated to ASML's node advancement.
- **Applied Materials (AMAT):** Etch/deposition equipment needed alongside ASML litho.
- **Lam Research (LRCX):** Same thesis as AMAT.
- **BESI (BESI.AS):** Advanced packaging equipment — post-litho value chain.

## Contrarian Angle

**What the market is missing:** The installed base. ASML has ~6,000+ lithography systems deployed globally. Each requires regular service, upgrades, and replacement parts. This installed base service revenue (~€6B/year) is growing and highly recurring — it provides a floor even in a downturn. The market prices ASML on new system sales volatility but undervalues the annuity stream.

**Second contrarian angle:** High-NA isn't just incremental. It enables chip architectures that weren't possible before (backside power delivery, new transistor geometries). This could EXPAND the total addressable market for litho rather than just replacing existing tools.

## Intelligence Summary

ASML is the ultimate "picks and shovels" play on AI. It doesn't matter which AI model wins, which cloud provider dominates, or which chip company leads — they ALL need ASML machines. The High-NA transition creates a 3-5 year upgrade cycle with 75% higher ASPs. Record bookings confirm demand isn't slowing. The only question is whether the stock's valuation already reflects this — at ~24x forward P/E for a monopoly growing 12-19%, we think there's still room.

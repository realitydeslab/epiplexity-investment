# CEG Competitive Map — Constellation Energy

## Direct Competitors

### Nuclear Power
- **No direct competitor at scale.** CEG operates the largest nuclear fleet in the US (~32 GW). No other company comes close.
- **NextEra Energy (NEE):** Largest utility by market cap but focused on renewables (wind/solar). Not a nuclear competitor.
- **Duke Energy (DUK), Southern Company (SO):** Have some nuclear plants but primarily regulated utilities. Different business model.
- **Talen Energy (TLN):** Smaller nuclear operator, sold Susquehanna data center rights to Amazon. Competitor in data center nuclear space.

### AI Data Center Power
- **Vistra Energy (VST):** Nuclear + gas fleet. Direct competitor for data center contracts. CEG's Calpine deal partly a response to Vistra's position.
- **Talen Energy (TLN):** Amazon/AWS nuclear deal. Smaller but positioned.
- **GE Vernova (GEV):** Makes nuclear turbines and grid equipment — supplier, not competitor.

### Natural Gas (Post-Calpine)
- **NRG Energy (NRG):** Large merchant power generator. Direct competitor in ERCOT/PJM markets.
- **AES Corporation (AES):** Diversified power but smaller scale.

## Who Benefits from Same Catalysts?
- **VST, OKLO, LEU, CCJ, SMR, NuScale** — all benefit from nuclear renaissance / AI power demand
- **GEV** — supplies equipment for nuclear and gas plants
- **AEIS** — power delivery systems for data centers

## Who Loses if CEG Wins?
- **Renewable-only companies** if nuclear gets policy preference over wind/solar
- **Fossil fuel opposition** — coal/gas plants without nuclear capability
- **Smaller IPPs** who can't offer 24/7 carbon-free baseload

## Pair Trade Opportunities
1. **Long CEG / Short NEE** — Nuclear ascendancy vs. renewable premium compression
2. **Long CEG + VST** — Nuclear power basket for AI theme
3. **Long CEG / Short NRG** — CEG has nuclear moat, NRG doesn't

## CEG's Unique Position
After Calpine: ~58 GW combined fleet (nuclear + gas + geothermal + renewables). This is unmatched in the US:
- **Nuclear baseload** (24/7 carbon-free) — hyperscalers want this
- **Gas peaking** (flexible dispatchable) — grid needs this
- **Geothermal** (always-on renewable) — adds to clean portfolio
- **Scale** — can offer GW-scale contracts no one else can match

**Moat: The nuclear fleet cannot be replicated.** It would take 15-20 years and $100B+ to build what CEG already owns. This is a permanent competitive advantage.

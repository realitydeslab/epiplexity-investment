## EPS Trajectory
| Year | EPS (Diluted) | YoY Growth |
|------|--------------|------------|
| FY2023 | $5.01 | — |
| FY2024 | $11.89 | +137% |
| FY2025 | $7.40 | -38% |
| FY2026E | $11.63 | +57% |
| FY2027E | $13.64 | +17% |

**Note:** FY2025 EPS decline is MISLEADING. It reflects mark-to-market hedging losses and Calpine deal costs, not operational deterioration. Underlying operating performance remained strong. FY2026 consensus expects a snap-back to $11.63 — basically FY2024 levels but on a structurally higher base.

## Guidance vs. Actual
- CEG has a strong beat-and-raise track record since the 2022 spin-off
- FY2024 was a massive beat year (EPS $11.89 vs. much lower initial guidance)
- Management guides conservatively and over-delivers

## Margins
| Metric | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Gross Margin | 13.0% | 25.4% | 18.4% |
| Operating Margin | 6.5% | 18.5% | 12.1% |
| EBITDA Margin | 16.6% | 29.9% | 22.3% |
| Profit Margin | 6.3% | 15.9% | 9.1% |

**Trend:** FY2025 margins compressed vs. FY2024 peak (mark-to-market effects), but still well above FY2023 levels. Structural margin improvement is intact.

## Path Forward: Calpine Impact
- Calpine adds ~$13B revenue and significant EBITDA
- Combined entity: ~$40B+ revenue, diversified across nuclear, gas, geothermal, renewables
- Accretive to EPS from day 1 of integration
- Gas peaking assets complement nuclear baseload — better pricing power

## Free Cash Flow
| Year | FCF | FCF/Share |
|------|-----|-----------|
| FY2023 | -$7.7B | -$23.84 |
| FY2024 | -$5.0B | -$15.97 |

**Inflection!** FCF turned positive in FY2025 after years of negative FCF (nuclear fuel purchases, capex). This is a critical inflection point — the business is now self-funding.

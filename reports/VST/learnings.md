# VST Structured Intelligence — Vistra Energy

*Research date: March 7, 2026*

## What This Tells Us About the Sector

1. **Nuclear is the new oil** — Hyperscalers will pay massive premiums for 24/7 carbon-free baseload power. Nuclear operators have pricing power they haven't had in decades.

2. **Integrated > Pure-play for risk-adjusted returns** — CEG gets the "nuclear pure-play" premium, but Vistra's integrated model (nuclear + gas + retail) provides downside protection. In a bear case, the retail business and gas fleet still generate significant FCF.

3. **M&A is the growth lever** — New nuclear plants take 10-15 years. The only way to grow nuclear capacity quickly is uprates (small) or buying existing assets (what Vistra is doing). Expect more consolidation.

4. **Texas is ground zero for AI power demand** — ERCOT's deregulated, competitive market + business-friendly environment = data center magnet. Vistra's Texas dominance is a structural advantage.

## Contrarian Angle — What's the Market Missing?

**The market is pricing VST as "nuclear hype" but missing the cash flow machine underneath.**

- At ~$166, VST trades at ~10x 2026E EBITDA — cheaper than most utilities
- $3.5B+ annual FCF with $1B+ buybacks = you're getting paid to wait
- The Cogentrix deal adds 5,500 MW at $730/kW — well below replacement cost ($1,500+/kW for new gas)
- If 2026 EBITDA hits $7.5B+, the stock is trading at <9x — absurdly cheap for a company with hyperscaler contracts

**What could go right that nobody's pricing:**
- Vistra Vision separation could unlock 30%+ value
- New nuclear PPAs with Google/Microsoft at even higher rates than AWS/Meta deals
- ERCOT capacity mechanism changes that reward dispatchable power

**What worries the market (and keeps the stock cheap):**
- Heavy insider selling ($227M in 12 months)
- "Has it already run?" narrative (7x in 3 years)
- AI capex deceleration fears
- Integration risk from rapid deal-making

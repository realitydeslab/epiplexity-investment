**Verdict: Growth is DECELERATING in percentage terms but ACCELERATING in absolute dollars.** This is the natural maturation from "discovery phase" (market realizes nuclear = AI gold) to "execution phase" (contracted cash flows compound).

## Growth Acceleration Drivers

### Generation Capacity Growth
- **Pre-2024:** ~36 GW total fleet
- **Post Energy Harbor (2024):** +~4 GW nuclear
- **Post Lotus (2025):** +2,600 MW gas
- **Post Cogentrix (2026):** +5,500 MW gas
- **Nuclear uprates:** +433 MW
- **Total 2026 fleet:** ~49+ GW — massive scale advantage

### Contract Quality Improving
- Nuclear PPAs with AWS and Meta at **premium pricing** (data center willingness-to-pay far exceeds wholesale)
- 3.8 GW nuclear contracted long-term = predictable, high-margin revenue
- Shift from merchant/spot exposure to contracted = lower risk, potentially higher valuation multiple

### Buyback-Driven EPS Growth
- ~30% share count reduction since 2021
- At $1B+/year buybacks at ~$166/share, that's ~1.8% of float per quarter
- FCF yield of ~6% at current price — significant for a "growth" story

## Unit Economics

- Nuclear plants have near-zero marginal fuel cost — once contracted, margins are exceptional
- Retail segment (5M+ customers) provides natural hedge and stable cash flow
- Integrated model smooths volatility vs. pure merchant generators
- Cogentrix acquisition at ~$730/kW net of tax benefits — well below replacement cost

## Path to Further Growth

Vistra is already profitable and cash-generative. The question is rate of growth:
- **Near-term (2026):** Cogentrix integration + nuclear PPA ramp = $6.8-7.6B EBITDA
- **Medium-term (2027-2028):** Full Cogentrix contribution + potential new nuclear PPAs = could push toward $8-9B EBITDA
- **Long-term:** If nuclear fleet fully contracted at premium rates, EBITDA could exceed $10B

## Risk to Growth
- Power price normalization (if natural gas prices drop sharply)
- Nuclear regulatory risk (unlikely but existential)
- Data center demand slowdown (the "AI winter" scenario)
- Integration risk from rapid M&A pace

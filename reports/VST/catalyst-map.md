# VST Catalyst Map — Vistra Energy

*Research date: March 7, 2026*

## 1. Cogentrix Energy Acquisition Close (~Q2-Q3 2026)
- **What:** $4.7B acquisition of 5,500 MW natural gas generation (10 facilities across PJM, ISO-NE, ERCOT)
- **When:** Expected mid-2026 (announced Jan 5, 2026)
- **Impact:** Mid-single digit AFCF/share accretion in 2027, high single-digit on average 2027-2029. Could add $500M+ EBITDA annually. Stock +5-10% on close confirmation.
- **Probability:** High — definitive agreement signed, regulatory approval in progress
- **Market expectation:** Largely priced in at deal value; execution risk still discounted
- **Our edge:** Market underestimates portfolio synergies — Cogentrix gas assets complement nuclear PPAs by providing dispatchable backup. Combined fleet makes Vistra THE integrated power provider for hyperscalers.

## 2. Additional Hyperscaler Nuclear PPAs (Q2-Q4 2026)
- **What:** New long-term nuclear power purchase agreements beyond AWS and Meta. Potential deals with Google, Microsoft, or Oracle.
- **When:** Ongoing through 2026
- **Impact:** +5-8% per major PPA announcement. Each GW contracted at premium rates adds ~$200-300M annual revenue.
- **Probability:** High — 3.8 GW already contracted, remaining nuclear capacity is scarce
- **Market expectation:** Some additional deals expected but not specific terms
- **Our edge:** With ~6.4 GW total nuclear and 3.8 GW contracted, remaining uncontracted nuclear is extremely scarce. Bidding war between hyperscalers could drive premium pricing.

## 3. 2026 EBITDA Guidance Beat (Q1-Q2 earnings, May/Aug 2026)
- **What:** Management guided $6.8B-$7.6B EBITDA for 2026. History of beating guidance.
- **When:** Q1 earnings ~May 2026, Q2 earnings ~Aug 2026
- **Impact:** Each beat drives +3-5%. Full-year tracking above $7.5B triggers analyst upgrades.
- **Probability:** High — management conservative; 2025 beat at $5.9B
- **Market expectation:** Consensus likely mid-range ~$7.1-7.2B
- **Our edge:** Lotus (2,600 MW) already integrated; nuclear PPAs ramping; power prices elevated. Could track toward high end.

## 4. Nuclear Uprate Completion (433 MW, H2 2026)
- **What:** Technical upgrades to PJM nuclear plants adding 433 MW carbon-free capacity — contracted to Meta
- **When:** Throughout 2026, completing H2 2026
- **Impact:** +2-3% as milestones hit. Proof of concept for further uprates.
- **Probability:** Medium-High — technical risk manageable but nuclear timelines can slip
- **Market expectation:** Priced in at current levels
- **Our edge:** Successful uprates open door to further capacity additions at existing plants, far cheaper than new builds.

## 5. Share Buyback Continuation ($1B+ annually)
- **What:** ~30% of shares retired since 2021. Committed to at least $1B/year buybacks + $300M dividends.
- **When:** Ongoing throughout 2026
- **Impact:** Steady EPS accretion of 5-7% annually from buybacks alone. Floor under stock.
- **Probability:** Very High — management committed, strong FCF supports it
- **Market expectation:** Priced in but underappreciated
- **Our edge:** At ~$166, buybacks are more accretive than at $200+.

## 6. Potential Vistra Vision Separation (Speculative, H2 2026)
- **What:** Separation of zero-carbon assets (nuclear, solar, battery) into standalone entity
- **When:** Could be announced anytime; management has discussed optionality
- **Impact:** +15-25% — nuclear pure-plays trade at massive premiums (see CEG)
- **Probability:** Low-Medium
- **Market expectation:** Not priced in
- **Our edge:** Sum-of-parts analysis suggests Vistra Vision alone could be worth more than entire current market cap.

## 7. Texas ERCOT Power Market Tightness (Summer 2026)
- **What:** Hot Texas summer + data center load growth = tight grid, elevated power prices
- **When:** June-September 2026
- **Impact:** +5-10% during summer if prices spike
- **Probability:** Medium-High — structural demand growth is real
- **Market expectation:** Some seasonal premium expected
- **Our edge:** New data center load in Texas is structural, not cyclical. Each summer gets tighter.

## 8. Index Rebalancing / Re-Rating
- **What:** Growing S&P 500 weight. Utility-to-infrastructure re-rating by analysts.
- **When:** Quarterly rebalances
- **Impact:** +2-3% from passive flows
- **Probability:** Medium

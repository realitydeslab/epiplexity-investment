# Strategic Intelligence: Alibaba Group (9988.HK)

*Research Date: March 7, 2026*

---

## What This Tells Us About the Sector

1. **China AI is real and accelerating.** Triple-digit AI cloud revenue growth for 9 consecutive quarters at China's largest cloud provider is not a blip. The DeepSeek effect has democratized AI in China and driven enterprise adoption to Alibaba Cloud.

2. **Open-source AI is winning.** Qwen's 180K derivative models vs competitors shows that open-source is the dominant strategy for AI ecosystem building. Meta (Llama) and Alibaba (Qwen) are the clear leaders. This strategy converts developer adoption into cloud revenue.

3. **The "China tech discount" may be a mispricing.** Alibaba trades at ~20x forward earnings with 34% cloud growth. Microsoft trades at 35x with Azure growing ~30%. The discount exists for real reasons (regulation, geopolitics) but may be too steep for the underlying fundamentals.

4. **AI infrastructure spending is global.** RMB120B ($16.5B) in capex over 4 quarters mirrors US hyperscaler spending patterns. This benefits the entire AI supply chain — NVIDIA, TSMC, Broadcom, and AI energy plays.

## Contrarian Angle: What's the Market Missing?

1. **Qwen's developer ecosystem is being undervalued.** 180K derivative models growing exponentially creates a lock-in effect similar to NVIDIA's CUDA moat but for AI models. The market treats Alibaba as an e-commerce company with a cloud side business. It's becoming a cloud/AI company with an e-commerce cash cow.

2. **The buyback is massive and accelerating.** $19.3B remaining at a $400B market cap. If they accelerate (which Eddie Wu's urgency suggests), 5-7% of shares could be retired in 12 months. This creates a mechanical floor.

3. **March 19 earnings could be a re-rating event.** If cloud growth hits 35-40% and AI revenue becomes >30% of cloud, analysts will need to reclassify Alibaba from "value e-commerce" to "growth AI infrastructure." That's a PE re-rating from 20x to 25-30x.

# SNOW — Strategic Intelligence & Learnings

*As of March 7, 2026*

## What This Tells Us About the Sector

1. **Enterprise AI adoption is real and accelerating.** Snowflake's 30% growth on a $4.7B base, driven by AI workloads, confirms enterprise AI spend is not just hype. The jump from 2 to 7 nine-figure contracts in one quarter is a leading indicator.

2. **Data infrastructure is the picks-and-shovels of enterprise AI.** Before companies can deploy AI, they need their data unified, governed, and accessible. Snowflake sits at this chokepoint.

3. **Multi-model AI is winning.** Snowflake's partnerships with Anthropic, OpenAI, AND Google show enterprises don't want to bet on one model provider. The "Switzerland" strategy is resonating.

4. **Consumption-based models create natural AI tailwinds.** Every AI query = more compute consumed. As AI workloads grow, consumption-based revenue accelerates without new contracts needed.

## Adjacent Plays

- **Confluent (CFLT):** Real-time streaming data feeds AI models — complementary to Snowflake's batch analytics
- **MongoDB (MDB):** Document database for AI application backends — different data shape, same AI tailwind
- **Elastic (ESTC):** Vector search/RAG for AI — search infrastructure layer
- **Fivetran (Private):** Data integration feeds Snowflake — acquisition target?

## Contrarian Angle

**What the market is missing:** The selloff has priced SNOW like growth is decelerating, but it's actually accelerating (26%→30%). The 40% drawdown from highs is a sector-wide SaaS punishment, not Snowflake-specific. At ~12x forward revenue (down from 20x+), the market is paying a "value" multiple for a company growing 30% with improving FCF margins. If AI workloads compound, this re-rates fast.

**The bear counter-argument:** SBC is massive, GAAP profitability is nowhere close, and Databricks is a formidable competitor that could IPO and steal the narrative. The consumption model also works in reverse — if enterprises cut back, revenue shrinks immediately.

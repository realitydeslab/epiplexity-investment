## The Catalyst

Enterprise AI adoption is inflecting. Snowflake's Q4 FY2026 showed 30% revenue growth (accelerating from 26%), a record $400M deal, and 7 nine-figure contracts (up from 2). AI workloads — Cortex AI, Snowflake Intelligence, Cortex Code — are becoming the growth engine. RPO of $9.77B (+42% YoY) signals massive contracted future revenue. The Observe acquisition opens a $50B observability TAM. Multi-model partnerships (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) make Snowflake the enterprise AI "Switzerland."

## The Trade

- **Entry:** ~$168 (down 40%+ from 52-week highs)
- **Target (base):** $225 (+34%) — meets FY2027 guidance
- **Target (bull):** $280 (+67%) — AI workloads beat, NRR re-accelerates
- **Stop-loss:** $145 (-14%)
- **Position:** 3-4% of portfolio

At ~12x forward revenue with 30% growth and improving FCF ($782M, +61% YoY), Snowflake is trading at a rare discount for a high-quality compounder. The SaaS selloff has created a valuation gap the market usually corrects within 6-12 months.

## The Risk

1. **Databricks competition** — especially if they IPO and steal AI/ML narrative
2. **GAAP losses** — SBC dilution is real, GAAP profitability is years away
3. **Consumption model reversal** — if enterprises cut AI budgets, revenue shrinks fast
4. **Lawsuits** — securities class actions create headline risk
5. **Macro** — if rates stay high, growth multiples compress further

## The Verdict

**BUY on weakness.** Snowflake is a best-in-class data infrastructure company entering its AI era. Growth is accelerating, FCF is expanding, and the stock is down 40% from highs in an indiscriminate selloff. The risk/reward at $168 is compelling — $10K becomes $13.4K (base) to $16.7K (bull) in 12 months, with downside limited to ~$8K with a disciplined stop.

# TSLA Growth Velocity Analysis

## Revenue Trajectory
| Period | Revenue | YoY Change |
|--------|---------|------------|
| FY2023 | $96.8B | +19% |
| FY2024 | ~$97.7B | +1% (deceleration) |
| FY2025 | ~$94.7B | **-3.1%** (first-ever annual decline) |
| Q4 2025 | $24.9B | -16% QoQ |

**Verdict: DECELERATING on automotive. But shifting revenue mix is the story.**

## Automotive vs Energy Split
- **Automotive FY2025:** Down ~11% YoY — EV Winter, brand headwinds, competition from BYD/Chinese EVs
- **Energy FY2025:** $12.8B (+26.6% YoY) — record MegaPack/Powerwall. Record quarterly gross profit
- Energy is now ~13.5% of revenue and growing fast

## Margins
| Metric | Q3 2025 | Q4 2025 | Trend |
|--------|---------|---------|-------|
| Auto gross margin (ex-credits) | 15.4% | 17.9% | ↑ Improving |
| Total gross margin | ~18% | 20.1% | ↑ Highest in 2+ years |
| Operating margin | ~6.2% | ~5.7% | ↓ Slight decline (opex up) |

## Deliveries
- Q4 2025: ~418K vehicles delivered, ~434K produced
- FY2025 total deliveries: Down from FY2024
- **But:** Larger backlog entering 2026, strong APAC/EMEA demand in smaller markets

## Guidance vs Actuals
- Tesla beat Q4 2025 EPS: $0.50 vs $0.45 consensus (slight beat)
- Revenue beat: $24.9B vs $24.77B expected
- Historically: Tesla has been a mixed beater — big misses on delivery guidance, but financial beats
- **FY2026 capex guidance:** >$20B for factories, fleet expansion, and AI compute

## FSD / Software Revenue
- 1.1M paid FSD customers worldwide
- Transitioning to subscription model — short-term margin headwind, long-term recurring revenue
- 70% were outright purchases, now shifting

## Unit Economics
- Average selling price still declining due to price cuts
- But margin improvement Q3→Q4 suggests worst of price war may be over
- Energy unit economics improving significantly (MegaPack margins expanding)

## Path to Growth Re-acceleration
1. **Robotaxi revenue** (new revenue stream, not cannibalizing auto)
2. **CyberCab at $30K** (opens lower price point without destroying margin)
3. **Energy** continuing 25%+ growth
4. **FSD subscriptions** reaching escape velocity
5. **Optimus** (2027+ revenue, but factory conversion starts 2026)

## Verdict
Growth is technically decelerating on the legacy auto business, but Tesla is in a **revenue mix transition**. The automotive decline masks acceleration in energy, FSD software, and soon robotaxi services. By Q3 2026, if robotaxi revenue materializes, total company growth could re-accelerate to double digits.

# TSLA Competitive Map

## Robotaxi / Autonomous Driving
| Competitor | Approach | Status | Threat Level |
|-----------|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Waymo (Alphabet)** | LiDAR + cameras, geo-mapped cities | Live in SF, LA, Phoenix. Expanding | HIGH — proven, but expensive to scale |
| **Cruise (GM)** | LiDAR + cameras | Recovering from 2024 shutdown, restructured | LOW — setback, but well-funded |
| **Baidu Apollo** | Full-stack, China focus | Live in 10+ Chinese cities | MEDIUM — China only, but massive scale |
| **Mobileye (Intel)** | Supplier model, SuperVision | Deployed via OEMs | MEDIUM — different business model |

**Tesla's edge:** Camera-only = cheaper per vehicle. Existing fleet of millions = data advantage. CyberCab purpose-built = lower cost.
**Tesla's weakness:** Regulatory risk higher (camera-only perceived as less safe), Waymo has safety lead in public perception.

## Humanoid Robots
| Competitor | Robot | Status |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Figure AI** | Figure 02 | BMW deployment, $30K-$250K range |
| **1X Technologies** | Neo | OpenAI-backed, $20K target or $499/mo |
| **Agility Robotics** | Digit | Amazon warehouse deployments |
| **Boston Dynamics** | Atlas | Most advanced but no mass production plan |

**Tesla's edge:** Manufacturing scale (Fremont 1M capacity), vertically integrated AI stack, cost advantages
**Tesla's weakness:** Late to deploy, competitors already in commercial pilots

## Pair Trade Opportunities
1. **Long TSLA / Short traditional OEMs (F, GM)** — if autonomy wins, legacy auto loses
2. **Long TSLA + Long GOOGL** — both win if robotaxi TAM expands (Waymo + Tesla duopoly)
3. **Long TSLA / Short UBER** — robotaxi replaces ride-hailing; UBER is the disrupted incumbent

## Who Benefits from Same Catalysts?
- **NVDA** — Tesla's AI compute supplier (Dojo + NVIDIA GPUs for training)
- **GOOGL** — Waymo validates robotaxi TAM alongside Tesla
- **Energy suppliers (CEG, VST)** — data center + EV charging power demand

## Who Loses if Tesla Wins?
- **UBER/LYFT** — robotaxi disrupts ride-hailing economics
- **Traditional auto (F, GM, Stellantis)** — if CyberCab at $30K captures volume
- **Auto dealers** — direct-to-consumer + autonomous fleet = no dealers needed

# Nebius Group (NBIS) — Growth Velocity

## Revenue Growth (Quarterly)

| Quarter | Revenue | YoY Growth | QoQ Growth |
|---------|---------|------------|------------|
| Q1 2025 | ~$50M (est) | ~150% | — |
| Q2 2025 | ~$100M (est) | ~300% | +100% |
| Q3 2025 | ~$146.1M | +237% | +46% |
| Q4 2025 | $227.7M | +547% | +56% |

**Verdict: ACCELERATING.** Both YoY and QoQ growth rates are increasing. Q4's 547% YoY and 56% QoQ are the fastest yet.

## Full-Year 2025
- **Total revenue:** ~$525M (company lowered from higher initial guidance due to capacity constraints — couldn't meet demand, not demand weakness)
- **ARR exit rate:** $1.25B (annualizing Q4 run-rate exceeds this)

## 2026 Guidance
- **Revenue:** $3.0–3.4B (reported revenue, not ARR)
- **ARR target:** $7–9B by end of CY2026
- **Over half of ARR target already contracted**

## Growth Rate Implied
- 2025 → 2026 revenue growth: ~475–550% (from ~$525M to $3.0–3.4B)
- This would make NBIS one of the fastest-growing companies at scale in history

## Customer/Capacity Trajectory
- **Active power:** 170 MW (end of 2025)
- **Target:** 800MW–1GW connected by end of 2026
- **Contracted:** 2GW+ (demand far exceeds current supply)
- Revenue is supply-constrained, not demand-constrained

## Order Backlog / Pipeline
- Microsoft: $17.4B (5-year, potentially $19.4B)
- Meta: Multi-year dedicated infrastructure deal (size undisclosed)
- Total backlog: **$20B+** across all contracts
- Volozh: "I believe there are more to come"

## Guidance vs. Actual History
- Q4 2025 revenue: **missed** ($227.7M vs $247M expected) — but due to capacity timing, not demand
- 2025 full-year guidance was **lowered** mid-year — again, supply-side constraint
- ARR guidance was **beaten** ($1.25B vs prior targets)
- Pattern: Under-delivers on reported revenue (capacity-gated) but over-delivers on contracted pipeline

## Unit Economics
- **Improving:** EBITDA margin expanded from negative → 24% in Q4 2025
- **Target:** ~40% EBITDA margin in 2026
- GPU rental prices reportedly **rising** (not falling), contracts **lengthening**
- Customer prepayments help finance capex, improving cash conversion

## Path to Profitability
- Q4 2025: First quarter of positive EBITDA
- 2026: Expected to achieve double-digit returns on capital
- Net loss in Q4 2025 ($249.6M) driven by massive capex depreciation and buildout costs
- At 40% EBITDA margins on $3B+ revenue = $1.2B+ EBITDA in 2026

## Key Risk: The Capex Mountain
- 2025 capex: $4.1B
- 2026 planned capex: **$16–20B**
- This is the single biggest concern — but largely financed by:
  - Customer prepayments
  - Contract-secured debt (Microsoft's credit quality enhances terms)
  - Potential equity/debt raises
- If demand softens or contracts get renegotiated, the capex becomes a liability

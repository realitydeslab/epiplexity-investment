# Nebius Group (NBIS) — Structured Intelligence

## What This Tells Us About the Sector
- **AI infrastructure demand is real and accelerating.** Nebius's $20B+ backlog and 547% revenue growth confirm that enterprise/hyperscaler GPU demand far exceeds supply.
- **The "GPU cloud" layer is emerging as a distinct market.** Between Nebius, CoreWeave, Lambda — a new category exists between chip makers (NVIDIA) and end-users (AI labs). This is the "AI factory" thesis.
- **Power is the new bottleneck.** Nebius's growth is gated by megawatts, not demand. This validates the AI energy thesis (VST, CEG, OKLO, LEU).
- **Hyperscalers are outsourcing, not insourcing.** Microsoft's $17.4B deal with Nebius proves that even the largest cloud providers need external GPU capacity. This trend may persist for years.

## Contrarian Angle — What's the Market Missing?
1. **The Russia discount is dead but priced in.** Volozh renounced Russian citizenship. Nebius is a Dutch company listed on Nasdaq with US data centers. Yet institutional investors still avoid it due to "Yandex" association. This creates a valuation gap.
2. **Capex isn't the risk it appears.** Bears focus on $16–20B capex. But this is largely pre-financed by customer prepayments and contract-secured debt. Nebius isn't speculating on demand — it's building to order.
3. **Avride is a free option.** The autonomous driving subsidiary has real technology (years of Moscow/US testing) and is currently valued at ~$0 in the stock price.
4. **The "AI spending bust" narrative doesn't apply here.** Nebius has $20B+ in contracted backlog. Even if new demand slows, existing contracts carry years of revenue.

- 1+ additional hyperscaler contract announced

- Valued at ~3x forward ARR = $21–27B+ market cap → $180–220/share

### Base Case ($150)
- Revenue tracks to $3B but ARR ramp slower than guided ($5B)
- Margins at 30% (below target but improving)
- No major new contracts beyond existing
- Steady re-rating as execution proves out

### Bear Case ($55)
- Capex explodes ($20B+) but capacity comes online late
- GPU rental prices decline as competition increases
- AI spending cycle cools — hyperscalers renegotiate or delay
- Stock trades at 1x forward revenue of ~$3B on $55B diluted basis → $55

## Risk Management
- **Stop-loss:** $75 (-25% from $100). Below 200 DMA breaks the technical thesis.
- **Position sizing:** 3–5% of portfolio. This is high-beta, high-conviction — not a core holding.
- **What kills the trade:**
  1. Q1 2026 revenue materially below expectations (would signal capacity delays are real)
  2. Hyperscaler contract cancellation or renegotiation
  3. GPU rental prices collapse (CoreWeave, Lambda undercutting)
  4. Dilutive equity raise at poor terms
- **Correlation risk:** NBIS is correlated to NVDA, CoreWeave (CRWV), and AI capex sentiment broadly. A correction in AI infrastructure names would drag NBIS regardless of fundamentals.

# Nebius Group (NBIS) — Catalyst Map

## 1. Revenue Ramp to $7–9B ARR by End of 2026
- **What:** Nebius targets $7–9B annualized run-rate revenue by end of CY2026, up from $1.25B ARR at end of 2025. Over half already contracted.
- **When:** Quarterly milestones throughout 2026; full-year results Feb 2027
- **Impact:** +100–200% stock move if trajectory confirms. Missing would be -40–50%.
- **Probability:** Medium-High — Microsoft ($17.4B) and Meta deals underpin, but execution on capacity buildout is the bottleneck.
- **Market expectation:** Consensus expects $3–3.4B reported revenue in 2026.
- **Our edge:** Market underestimates how fast contracted capacity converts to revenue once data centers come online.

## 2. Microsoft $17.4B Deal — Revenue Recognition Begins
- **What:** Multi-year deal to deliver dedicated GPU infrastructure from Vineland, NJ data center. Total could reach $19.4B with additional services.
- **When:** Revenue recognition starting Q1–Q2 2026 as Vineland comes online
- **Impact:** +20–30% on first material quarterly revenue from this contract
- **Probability:** High — contract signed, facility in late-stage construction
- **Market expectation:** Priced in at deal level, but execution cadence is not
- **Our edge:** First quarter showing $500M+ run-rate from Microsoft alone would force analyst upgrades

## 3. Data Center Capacity — 800MW to 1GW Connected in 2026
- **What:** From 170MW active at end of 2025, targeting 800MW–1GW connected by end of 2026. 2GW+ contracted. Missouri 1.2GW campus being built.
- **When:** Phased through 2026; major capacity additions likely Q2–Q3
- **Impact:** Revenue is directly gated by connected megawatts. Each 100MW ≈ ~$800M–1B+ ARR potential.
- **Probability:** Medium — construction, permitting, power procurement are real risks
- **Market expectation:** Skepticism on execution pace is the main bear case
- **Our edge:** Nebius beat initial capacity targets (hit 170MW ahead of schedule)

## 4. EBITDA Margin Expansion to ~40%
- **What:** Core adjusted EBITDA margin expanded to 24% in Q4 2025. Targeting ~40% in 2026 as scale improves unit economics.
- **When:** Gradual through 2026; should be visible by Q2 reports
- **Impact:** +30–50% if margins hit 40% at scale — proves this isn't a capital-destruction story
- **Probability:** Medium — depends on GPU rental pricing holding and operational efficiency
- **Market expectation:** Major skepticism due to $16–20B capex plan for 2026
- **Our edge:** Customer prepayments finance most capex; this is asset-light in disguise

## 5. Additional Hyperscaler Contracts
- **What:** Volozh signaled "more to come" after Microsoft deal. Meta already signed. Google, Oracle, or sovereign AI deals possible.
- **When:** Could be announced any quarter
- **Impact:** +15–30% per major contract announcement
- **Probability:** Medium-High
- **Market expectation:** Not priced in beyond existing contracts
- **Our edge:** Nebius is one of few scaled independent GPU cloud providers globally

## 6. Potential Index Inclusion (Russell 1000)
- **What:** Market cap ~$25.6B puts NBIS in range for major index inclusion
- **When:** Russell reconstitution June 2026
- **Impact:** +5–10% from passive fund buying
- **Probability:** Medium for Russell; Low for S&P 500 (needs sustained profitability)
- **Market expectation:** Not widely discussed

## 7. Avride Autonomous Driving Value Unlock
- **What:** Avride (ex-Yandex autonomous driving) could be spun off or attract strategic investment
- **When:** Potential 2H 2026
- **Impact:** +5–15% — hidden value not reflected in GPU cloud valuation
- **Probability:** Low-Medium
- **Market expectation:** Near-zero value assigned to Avride

## 8. Geopolitical De-risking — CEO Renounces Russian Citizenship
- **What:** Arkady Volozh officially renounced Russian citizenship (confirmed Feb 2026). Removes Yandex legacy overhang.
- **When:** Already happened — ongoing sentiment improvement
- **Impact:** +10–15% multiple expansion as ESG/sovereign concerns fade
- **Probability:** High — already done
- **Our edge:** Russia discount persists in sentiment but is now fundamentally unjustified

# Nebius Group (NBIS) — Leader Profile

## CEO: Arkady Volozh

### Background
- **Role:** Founder & CEO of Nebius Group (and previously Yandex)
- **Nationality:** Kazakh-born, Israeli citizen (renounced Russian citizenship Feb 2026)
- **Track record:** Built Yandex into Russia's dominant tech giant (~$30B+ peak market cap). After 2022 geopolitical fallout, orchestrated the $5.4B sale of Russian assets and pivoted remaining international assets into Nebius — a pure-play AI infrastructure company.

### Leadership Assessment
- **Visionary operator:** Volozh isn't a hype CEO. He built Yandex from scratch over 20+ years, including its own search engine, autonomous driving (now Avride), cloud, and AI/ML infrastructure. He understands full-stack tech deeply.
- **Crisis-tested:** Navigated the most complex corporate restructuring in recent tech history — splitting a $30B+ company across geopolitical fault lines while preserving shareholder value.
- **Execution credibility:** In 18 months post-relisting (Oct 2024), secured Microsoft $17.4B deal, Meta partnership, grew ARR from near-zero to $1.25B, and expanded from European data centers to US mega-campus buildouts.
- **Strategic clarity:** Clearly articulated the "AI factory" vision — build, fill, repeat — where contracted capacity finances buildout. This is a land-and-expand playbook.

### Insider Activity
- **Dec 2025:** Volozh filed Form 144 to sell 41,000 Class A shares (~$3.6M) — described as "sell-to-cover" for tax obligations. Two other insiders (Ophir, Bunina) sold 2,300 shares each. Shares dipped ~1.8% on the news.
- **Context:** This is routine tax-related selling, not conviction selling. $3.6M is <0.02% of his holdings. No large-scale insider selling pattern.
- **No insider buying:** No notable open-market purchases, though Volozh's economic interest is overwhelmingly via his existing stake.

### Key Hires / Departures
- No notable C-suite departures reported in last 6 months
- Engineering talent largely retained from Yandex's international teams (Europe, Israel)
- Hiring aggressively in data center operations and sales

### Public Statements (Near-term Outlook)
- Q4 2025 letter: "We have line of sight to $7–9B ARR... more than half is already contracted"
- On Microsoft deal: "The economics are attractive in their own right... I believe there are more to come"
- On capex: Company expects to finance $16–20B 2026 capex through customer prepayments and contract-secured debt

### Verdict
Volozh is the right leader for this phase. He's a builder, not a promoter. The risk is execution speed, not strategic direction. His small insider sale was tax-driven, not a red flag.

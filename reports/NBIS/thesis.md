# Nebius Group (NBIS) — Investment Thesis

## The One-Liner
Nebius is the fastest-growing AI infrastructure company at scale, with $20B+ contracted backlog, a Microsoft mega-deal, and a founder-CEO who built Yandex — trading at a geopolitical discount that no longer exists.

## The Catalyst
Revenue recognition from the $17.4B Microsoft deal begins in H1 2026. Combined with Meta and other contracts, Nebius is targeting $7–9B ARR by end of 2026 — a 6–7x increase from $1.25B at end of 2025. Over half is already contracted. The question isn't demand — it's whether they can build data centers fast enough.

## The Trade
- **Entry:** ~$100 (current price, near 50 DMA support)
- **Target:** $150–200 (12 months)
- **Stop:** $75 (below 200 DMA, -25%)
- **Position size:** 3–5% of portfolio

## The Risk
The $16–20B capex plan for 2026 is the biggest risk. If capacity comes online late, contracts get renegotiated, or GPU rental prices compress, the stock could revisit $55–70. The bear case is a capital-intensive business that never reaches profitability at scale. But: customer prepayments and contract-secured debt finance most capex, and Q4 2025 already showed positive EBITDA at 24% margins.

## The Verdict
**BUY.** This is a high-conviction, high-beta play on AI infrastructure demand. The risk/reward is asymmetric: $10K invested could become $15K–20K in the bull/base case, vs. $5.5K in the bear case. The Russia discount is fading, execution is proving out, and the contracted backlog provides unusual visibility for a growth stock. Scale in at current levels.

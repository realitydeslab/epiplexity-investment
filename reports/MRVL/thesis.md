*Research Date: March 6, 2026*

---

## The Catalyst
Marvell just reported record FY2026 results ($8.2B revenue, +42% YoY) and guided for **>30% revenue growth accelerating each quarter in FY2027**, targeting $11B+. The stock surged 15% after-hours. The real catalyst: **custom ASIC ramp + 1.6T optical interconnect + Celestial AI photonic fabric** create a triple growth engine with years of runway.

## The Trade
- **Entry:** $83-87 (post-gap consolidation zone)
- **12-month target:** $110 (base), $130 (bull)
- **Stop-loss:** $72
- **PEG ratio:** 0.66 — genuinely undervalued for 30%+ growth
- **Position size:** 3-5% of portfolio

## The Risk
- **AI capex slowdown** — if hyperscalers cut spending, the thesis breaks
- **NVIDIA encroachment** — Spectrum-X platform competes in interconnect
- **Integration risk** — Celestial AI/XConn need to deliver on schedule
- **Macro** — beta of 1.97 means this stock amplifies market moves; Iran conflict, rates staying higher
- **Valuation stretch** — at $130, P/E would be ~37x, requires continued execution

## The Verdict

**BUY on pullback.** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5 conviction)

Marvell is the **"picks and shovels" play** on the second wave of AI infrastructure — not the GPU sellers, but the plumbers connecting everything together. Matt Murphy has executed brilliantly, the forward guidance is the strongest in the company's history, and the PEG ratio says the market hasn't caught up yet.

The 15% after-hours pop means you're not getting the discount you'd want. Wait for consolidation ($83-85) or sell puts at $80 to build a position. If it holds above $85 for a week, buy with a $72 stop.

**Best for:** Investors who want AI exposure beyond NVIDIA, with a company demonstrating actual revenue acceleration and improving margins.

**Not for:** Conservative investors who can't stomach beta ~2 in a volatile macro environment.

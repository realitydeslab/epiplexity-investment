# TSM (TSMC) — Catalyst Map 🎯

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

## 1. N2 (2nm) GAA Ramp — Revenue Inflection

- **What:** TSMC began mass production of N2 (Gate-All-Around nanosheet) in Q4 2025. Ramping to full volume through 2026 with 50,000+ wafers/month already achieved. Apple and NVIDIA have locked up initial capacity.
- **When:** Ramp through all of 2026; volume revenue impact Q2-Q4 2026
- **Impact:** N2 carries premium ASPs (est. 20-30% above N3). Could add $3-5B incremental revenue in 2H 2026. +5-8% stock upside as ramp proves yields are strong.
- **Probability:** High — production already started, yields confirmed good
- **Market expectation:** Street models gradual N2 contribution; consensus ~$5B N2 revenue in 2026
- **Our edge:** Apple iPhone 18 + NVIDIA next-gen GPUs both on N2 create a demand surge that likely exceeds initial capacity. Upside to consensus N2 revenue.

## 2. CoWoS Advanced Packaging Expansion

- **What:** Scaling CoWoS capacity from ~35K wafers/month (late 2024) to 130K wafers/month by end of 2026. This was THE bottleneck for AI chip delivery.
- **When:** Continuous ramp through 2026, major capacity additions mid-year
- **Impact:** Removes the primary supply constraint for NVIDIA/AMD AI accelerators. Each CoWoS wafer generates very high revenue. Could unlock $8-12B in previously constrained AI chip revenue.
- **Probability:** High — capex already committed ($52-56B for 2026)
- **Market expectation:** Analysts model 30% revenue growth for 2026; CoWoS is a key enabler
- **Our edge:** The magnitude of CoWoS expansion (3.7x in 2 years) is under-appreciated. This turns TSMC from supply-constrained to demand-capturing.

## 3. Arizona Fab Profitability & Expansion

- **What:** TSMC Arizona (Fab 21) reported its first annual profit in 2025. $150B+ in tracked U.S. projects. Potential additional $100B investment being considered.
- **When:** Profitability milestone already hit (March 2026 report). Expansion decisions through 2026.
- **Impact:** De-risks the geopolitical narrative. Proves TSMC can manufacture profitably outside Taiwan. +3-5% re-rating as geopolitical discount narrows.
- **Probability:** High — already profitable
- **Market expectation:** Market still applies a 10-15% "Taiwan risk" discount
- **Our edge:** Arizona profitability is a paradigm shift. Each new fab announcement further reduces geopolitical overhang.

## 4. Q1 2026 Earnings (April)

- **What:** Guided Q1 revenue $34.6-35.8B (up 38% YoY at midpoint). Gross margin 63-65%.
- **When:** Mid-April 2026
- **Impact:** Beat-and-raise cycle continuation. Could push stock to new highs if margins expand. +3-5% on beat.
- **Probability:** High — TSMC has beaten estimates in 8 consecutive quarters
- **Market expectation:** Street at guided range (~$35.2B)
- **Our edge:** February monthly revenue was NT$401.26B (37% YoY growth), suggesting Q1 tracking strong.

## 5. AI Revenue 60% CAGR Confirmation

- **What:** Management guided AI chip revenue to grow at 60% CAGR through 2029. 2026 is the year this thesis gets validated or challenged.
- **When:** Quarterly earnings through 2026
- **Impact:** If AI revenue grows 60%+ in 2026, forward estimates get raised significantly. +10-15% re-rating potential.
- **Probability:** Medium-High — hyperscaler capex remains strong
- **Market expectation:** Consensus models ~30% total revenue growth (AI at 50-60% within that)
- **Our edge:** CoWoS unlocking + N2 ramp + NVIDIA Vera Rubin cycle = AI revenue likely at upper end

## 6. Capex Commitment ($52-56B) Signaling Future Growth

- **What:** 2026 capex of $52-56B, up 40% from 2025. 70-80% on advanced process.
- **When:** Spending throughout 2026
- **Impact:** As CFO Huang noted: "Higher capex correlates to high growth in following years." This signals 2027-2028 acceleration.
- **Probability:** High — capex already approved
- **Market expectation:** Priced in at current levels
- **Our edge:** The magnitude ($56B) positions TSMC to capture nearly ALL leading-edge demand. Competitors falling further behind.

## 7. Potential Tariff Exemptions

- **What:** U.S. government planning tariff exemptions for TSMC if it follows through on American investment
- **When:** 2026 (policy timeline uncertain)
- **Impact:** Removes cost uncertainty, improves margins on U.S. operations. +2-3% sentiment boost.
- **Probability:** Medium — dependent on political dynamics
- **Market expectation:** Not priced in

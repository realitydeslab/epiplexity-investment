## Key Growth Metrics

### AI Revenue
- AI chips at 60% CAGR through 2029 (management guidance)
- Advanced nodes (3nm + 5nm) = 63% of wafer revenue in Q4 2025
- AI/HPC mix increasing every quarter

### Monthly Revenue Momentum
- February 2026: NT$401.26B — **37% YoY growth** (record monthly revenue)
- This suggests Q1 2026 is tracking ABOVE the guided range

### Profitability — Expanding
- Q4 2025 Gross Margin: 62.3% (beating estimates)
- Q4 2025 Operating Margin: 54.0%
- Q4 2025 Net Income: NT$505.7B (+35% YoY)
- Q1 2026 Gross Margin Guide: 63-65% — **further expansion**
- Full-year 2025 EPS: NT$66.24 (+35% YoY)

### Guidance vs. Actual (Beat History)
- **8 consecutive quarters of earnings beats**
- Management consistently guides conservatively and over-delivers
- Q4 2025: Net income beat by NT$27B+ vs. estimates

## Customer/Demand Pipeline

- Apple: Locked up >50% of N2 initial capacity for iPhone 18
- NVIDIA: Vera Rubin cycle on N2/N2P; Blackwell still ramping on N3/N5
- AMD: MI400 on advanced nodes
- Broadcom, Qualcomm, MediaTek: All competing for capacity
- Hyperscalers (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Meta MTIA): Custom silicon growing rapidly

## Unit Economics

- N2 ASPs 20-30% above N3 → revenue per wafer improving
- CoWoS packaging adds significant margin per advanced chip
- Arizona profitability means U.S. operations no longer a drag
- Scale benefits: larger volumes on advanced nodes improve utilization

## Path to... Even More Profitability

TSMC is already highly profitable (54% operating margin). The path forward:
- N2 premium pricing → margin expansion
- CoWoS capacity unlocking → higher revenue per wafer
- Scale on $52-56B capex → operating leverage in 2027-2028
- Mix shift toward AI (higher ASP) continues

**Growth Velocity Score: 9/10** — One of the fastest-growing mega-caps in the world, with accelerating absolute revenue and expanding margins.

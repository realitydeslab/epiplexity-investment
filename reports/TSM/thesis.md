# TSM (TSMC) — Investment Thesis

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

---

## The Catalyst

TSMC is entering 2026 with **three simultaneous growth engines firing**: (1) N2/2nm mass production ramping with Apple and NVIDIA as anchor customers, (2) CoWoS advanced packaging expanding 3.7x to remove THE bottleneck for AI chip supply, and (3) Arizona fab profitability proving geographic diversification works. Management guides ~30% revenue growth and 63-65% gross margins — and they've beaten estimates 8 quarters straight.

## The Trade

**Entry:** $310-330 (current range, or pullbacks to support)
**12-month base target:** $400 (+21%)
**12-month bull target:** $450 (+36%)
**Stop-loss:** $275 (-17%)

This is a "strongest company in the world" trade. TSMC manufactures 71% of the world's leading-edge chips. There is no substitute. Every AI chip from NVIDIA, AMD, Broadcom, and custom hyperscaler designs flows through TSMC's fabs.

## The Risk

1. **Taiwan geopolitics** — The tail risk that never goes away. Mitigated (partially) by Arizona profitability and global diversification.
2. **AI capex cycle peaking** — If hyperscalers cut spending, TSMC's 60% AI CAGR breaks. Watch Q3-Q4 2026 guidance.
3. **Valuation stretch** — At ~22x forward earnings, not cheap for a cyclical semiconductor company. But the "cyclical" label may be wrong given structural AI demand.

## The Verdict

**BUY.** TSMC is the most critical company in the AI supply chain. The N2 ramp, CoWoS expansion, and Arizona profitability create a trifecta of catalysts in 2026. The 8-quarter beat streak likely extends. The market still applies a Taiwan discount that should narrow as geographic diversification progresses.

**$10K invested today → likely $12,000-13,600 in 12 months** (base-to-bull). Bear case ~$7,900 on AI demand shock or geopolitical escalation.

**Position size:** 5-8% of portfolio. High conviction, but tail risks warrant discipline.

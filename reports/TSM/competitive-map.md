# TSM (TSMC) — Competitive Map 🗺️

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

## Market Position

TSMC controls **68-71% of global semiconductor foundry revenue** (TrendForce, Q2 2025). This is one of the most dominant positions in any industry, anywhere.

## Direct Competitors

### Samsung Foundry (~8% market share)
- **Status:** Struggling with GAA yields; lost ground on 3nm
- **Threat level:** Low near-term. Samsung's GAA node has yield issues.
- **Watch for:** Any Samsung breakthrough on 2nm could signal long-term competition
- **TSMC advantage:** Yield, reliability, customer trust, scale

### Intel Foundry (Intel 18A)
- **Status:** Intel 18A in development, targeting external customers
- **Threat level:** Medium long-term. Intel has $150B+ in U.S. government support.
- **Watch for:** Intel 18A customer wins (beyond internal use)
- **TSMC advantage:** 2+ years ahead technologically, vastly more scale

### Rapidus (Japan)
- **Status:** Targeting mass 2nm production in 2027, 25K wafers/month
- **Threat level:** Low. Scale is tiny vs. TSMC's 50K+ N2 wafers already.
- **TSMC advantage:** Already in volume production; Rapidus is aspirational

## Who Benefits From Same Catalysts?

Companies that win if AI chip demand stays strong (same thesis):
- **ASML (ASML)** — EUV machines essential for N2. Monopoly supplier to TSMC.
- **NVIDIA (NVDA)** — Primary customer; TSMC capacity unlocks = NVIDIA revenue
- **AMD (AMD)** — Benefits from CoWoS expansion for MI400
- **Broadcom (AVGO)** — Custom ASICs manufactured at TSMC
- **Arm Holdings (ARM)** — CPU architecture in every TSMC AI chip

## Who Loses If TSMC Wins?

- **Samsung Foundry** — Loses more customers to TSMC's superior yields
- **Intel Foundry** — Harder to attract external customers when TSMC dominates
- **Chinese foundries (SMIC)** — Export controls + TSMC's lead = widening gap
- **GlobalFoundries** — Stuck at mature nodes, can't compete on leading edge

## Pair Trade Opportunities

1. **Long TSM / Short Samsung (005930.KS)** — TSMC taking share in advanced nodes
2. **Long TSM + Long ASML** — Stacked bet on leading-edge semiconductor spending
3. **Long TSM + Long NVDA** — Pure AI supply chain play (manufacturer + designer)

## Supply Chain Position

```
ASML (EUV tools) → TSMC (manufacturing) → NVIDIA/Apple/AMD (design) → Hyperscalers (deployment)
```

TSMC sits at the critical chokepoint. Every AI chip flows through TSMC. There is no alternative at scale for leading-edge manufacturing.

## Moat Assessment

| Moat Factor | Strength | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Technology lead | 🟢 Very Strong | 2+ years ahead of Samsung/Intel |
| Scale | 🟢 Very Strong | 71% market share, massive capex |
| Customer lock-in | 🟢 Very Strong | Switching costs are enormous |
| Ecosystem (CoWoS, InFO) | 🟢 Very Strong | Advanced packaging is a second moat |
| Geopolitical risk | 🔴 Weakness | Taiwan concentration |
| Pricing power | 🟢 Strong | N2 premium pricing accepted by all customers |

**Overall moat: Wide and widening.** Competitors are falling further behind, not catching up.

*Sources: TrendForce, Motley Fool, Tom's Hardware, industry analysis*

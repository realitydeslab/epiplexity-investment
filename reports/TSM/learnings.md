# TSM (TSMC) — Structured Intelligence 🧠

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

## What This Tells Us About the Sector

1. **AI capex cycle is NOT peaking.** TSMC's 40% capex increase ($52-56B) is the strongest signal that hyperscaler demand remains robust. You don't spend $56B if your customers are pulling back.

2. **Advanced packaging is the new bottleneck, not lithography.** CoWoS expansion from 35K to 130K wafers/month tells us that the constraint has shifted from chip fabrication to chip assembly. This has implications for packaging equipment makers (Disco, Kulicke & Soffa, ASE).

3. **The foundry market is becoming a monopoly.** At 71% share, TSMC has no real competitor at the leading edge. Samsung is struggling; Intel is years away. This is unprecedented concentration of manufacturing capability.

4. **Geographic diversification is real but slow.** Arizona profitability is a milestone, but >90% of production remains in Taiwan. Japan (Kumamoto) and Germany fabs won't contribute meaningfully until 2027-2028.

## Adjacent Plays

### Direct beneficiaries of TSMC's expansion:
- **ASML (ASML)** — Every new TSMC fab needs billions in EUV tools. $52-56B capex = massive ASML orders.
- **Applied Materials (AMAT)** — Deposition and etch equipment for N2 GAA
- **Tokyo Electron (8035.T)** — Coater/developer tools, major TSMC supplier
- **ASE Technology (ASX)** — OSAT (packaging) overflow from TSMC's CoWoS
- **Disco Corp (6146.T)** — Dicing/grinding equipment for advanced packaging

### Indirect beneficiaries:
- **Vistra/Constellation Energy** — AI data center power demand
- **Vertiv (VRT)** — Cooling for AI servers that TSMC's chips power
- **Arm Holdings (ARM)** — Architecture royalties on every chip

## Contrarian Angle — What's the Market Missing?

**The market underestimates TSMC's pricing power.** N2 wafer prices are 20-30% above N3, and customers are EAGER to pay. In a normal semiconductor cycle, customers push back on pricing. In the AI cycle, they're pre-paying for capacity. This pricing power translates directly to margin expansion that isn't fully modeled.

**The "Taiwan risk" discount is probably too high.** Arizona profitability + Japan fab + potential Europe fab = TSMC is actively building optionality. The 10-15% valuation discount vs. U.S. peers should narrow.

**The real risk isn't China invading Taiwan — it's AI demand peaking.** The geopolitical risk gets all the headlines, but the more probable risk is a cyclical slowdown in AI spending. If hyperscalers get ROI religion in 2H 2026, TSMC's growth could decelerate. Watch for any hyperscaler capex guidance cuts.

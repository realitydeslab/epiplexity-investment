# AMZN — The 1-Page Thesis

*Amazon.com, Inc. | $213.89 | March 6, 2026*

---

## The Catalyst

**AWS AI inflection + margin expansion.** Amazon is no longer just an e-commerce company — it's a $2.3T AI infrastructure + cloud + advertising conglomerate. AWS (~$110B run-rate) is the world's largest cloud platform and the primary on-ramp for enterprise AI. Massive 2025 capex ($75B+) unlocks capacity in 2026 just as AI demand explodes. Simultaneously, operating margins have gone from 2.4% (2022) to 11.2% (2025) and are still expanding.

## The Trade

- **Entry:** $213.89 (post-earnings gap-down, 31% below analyst consensus $279.57)
- **Target (12-month):** $260 (base), $295 (bull)
- **Stop-Loss:** $190 (-11%)
- **Risk/Reward:** ~2:1

**Why now?** Stock pulled back 13% from $245 highs on macro fears (tariffs, slowdown). Fundamentals are accelerating — revenue growth went from 9.4% (2022) to 13.6% (Q4 2025). Forward P/E of 27x is reasonable for a company growing EPS 30%/year.

## The Risk

- **AI capex trap:** $75B+ annual capex must generate returns; if AI monetization disappoints, FCF stays compressed
- **AWS deceleration:** If Azure/GCP take share, or enterprises slow cloud migration in recession
- **Macro:** Consumer recession hits e-commerce + enterprise budget cuts hit AWS simultaneously
- **Multiple compression:** Tech derating if rates stay higher for longer

## The Verdict

**BUY on weakness. $205-215 is the accumulation zone.**

Amazon is a rare mega-cap compounder with accelerating revenue growth, rapidly expanding margins, and multiple secular tailwinds (AI, ads, cloud). The post-earnings pullback creates an attractive entry. 44 analysts rate it Strong Buy with 31% upside to consensus.

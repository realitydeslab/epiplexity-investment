# AMZN Structured Intelligence 🧠

*Amazon.com, Inc. | Research Date: March 6, 2026*

## What This Tells Us About the Sector/Market

1. **AI capex cycle is REAL and MASSIVE** — Amazon spent ~$75B+ on capex in 2025, mostly AI infrastructure. This validates the AI infrastructure buildout thesis across the entire stack (NVDA, AVGO, VRT, CEG, etc.)

2. **Cloud margins are the new profit engine** — Amazon's transformation from 2.4% to 11.2% operating margin in 3 years was driven primarily by AWS profitability + cost cuts. This pattern will repeat at other cloud-dependent companies

3. **Advertising is the hidden story** — Amazon's ad business (~$65B+ run-rate) is now larger than YouTube's and approaching Meta's scale. High-intent shopping data is uniquely valuable. This validates the "first-party data + AI targeting" thesis

4. **FCF compression from AI investment is a feature, not a bug** — Companies spending heavily on AI infra (AMZN, MSFT, META, GOOGL) show temporarily depressed FCF. The market is starting to understand this is forward investment, not waste

## Adjacent Plays That Benefit from the Same Thesis

- **NVDA** — Supplies GPUs to AWS (though Trainium threatens this long-term)
- **VRT** — Cooling/power for Amazon's data centers
- **CEG/VST** — Power generation for AI data centers
- **ANET** — Networking equipment for AWS infrastructure
- **CLS** — Server manufacturing partner
- **MRVL** — Custom silicon and networking for cloud

## Contrarian Angle — What's the Market Missing?

1. **International profitability inflection** — Amazon International has been a money pit for years. If it reaches consistent profitability in 2026 (signs of this in recent quarters), it adds ~$5-10B operating income the market isn't modeling

2. **Alexa/AI assistant renaissance** — With LLMs, Amazon could transform Alexa from a joke into a genuinely useful AI assistant, driving Prime engagement and e-commerce. This is currently valued at ~$0

3. **Healthcare (Amazon Pharmacy/One Medical)** — $600B+ TAM that Amazon is quietly building into. AI-powered diagnostics + pharmacy delivery could be a dark horse by 2027

4. **The stock is cheap for what it is** — At 27x forward earnings with 14.5% revenue growth, 30% EPS growth, and multiple catalysts, AMZN is cheaper than many slower-growing tech names

# AVGO Growth Velocity — Broadcom

## Revenue Trajectory
| Quarter | Revenue | YoY Growth | AI Revenue | AI YoY |
|---------|---------|------------|------------|--------|
| Q4 FY2025 | ~$14.9B | +51% | ~$4.1B | +220% |
| Q1 FY2026 | $19.31B | +29.5% | $8.4B | +106% |
| Q2 FY2026 (guided) | ~$22.0B | ~+30% est. | ~$10B+ est. | ~+80% est. |

## Growth Acceleration Analysis
- **Total revenue growth decelerating slightly** (51% → 29.5%) — base effect from VMware acquisition lapping
- **AI revenue ACCELERATING in absolute terms:** $4.1B → $8.4B → $10B+ trajectory
- **AI as % of total:** Now ~43% of revenue and growing — will cross 50% by Q3-Q4 FY2026
- **Q2 guidance of $22B was $1.5B above consensus** ($20.5B) — massive guidance beat

## Guidance vs. Actual History
- Q1 FY2026: Guided ~$19.1B → Delivered $19.31B (+$210M beat)
- Pattern of conservative guidance followed by upside surprises
- Management credibility exceptionally high

## Unit Economics
- **Adjusted EBITDA margin:** 67% in Q1, guided 68% for Q2 (improving)
- **Free cash flow:** $8B in Q1 alone
- **Operating leverage:** AI revenue at higher margins than legacy business
- **VMware transition:** Shifting to subscriptions — structurally improves recurring revenue

## Path to Revenue Targets
- FY2026 revenue trajectory: ~$80-85B
- FY2027 target: $100B+ in AI chips alone (per CEO)
- If achieved, total FY2027 revenue could exceed $130B

## Verdict: ACCELERATING where it matters
AI revenue — the engine that matters — is on a steep acceleration curve. Growth velocity: **Strong.**

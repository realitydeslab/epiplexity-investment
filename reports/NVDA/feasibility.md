# NVDA Feasibility Report
**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Current Price:** ~$195 | **Market Cap:** ~$4.5T

## Financials Summary

| Metric | FY2026 | FY2025 | Growth |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Revenue | $215.9B | $130.5B | +65% |
| Gross Margin (non-GAAP) | 71.3% | 75.5% | -4.2 pts |
| Net Income (GAAP) | $120.1B | $72.9B | +65% |
| EPS (non-GAAP) | $4.77 | $2.99 | +60% |

**Valuation:** Trailing P/E ≈ 41x. Forward P/E on ~$6.50 FY27E ≈ 30x.

## Competitive Moat

1. **CUDA Ecosystem:** 20 years of software/tools/community. Deepest moat in semis.
2. **Annual Innovation Cadence:** Hopper → Blackwell → Blackwell Ultra → Vera Rubin.
3. **Full-Stack:** HW + networking + software + models. No competitor matches this.
4. **Scale:** $216B revenue funds massive R&D advantage.

## Competition

- **AMD (Instinct):** Value alternative, <10% AI accelerator share. Gains at margin.
- **Broadcom (Custom ASICs):** $60-90B TAM from hyperscaler custom chips. Real but workload-specific.
- **Intel:** Largely irrelevant in AI accelerators.
- **Hyperscaler In-House (TPU, Trainium, Maia):** Biggest long-term risk. But they still buy massive NVIDIA volumes alongside custom silicon.

## Key Risks

1. **Customer Concentration:** Top 4-5 hyperscalers = majority of DC revenue
2. **Gross Margin Compression:** FY26 margins dropped ~4 pts (HBM costs, Blackwell ramp)
3. **China Export Restrictions:** Already not in guidance, but geopolitical risk persists
4. **Cyclicality:** AI capex boom could slow if ROI unproven post-2027
5. **Valuation:** At $4.5T, small deceleration = big multiple compression

## Scenarios

**Bull (25%):** $280-320 — AI spending accelerates, Vera Rubin upgrade cycle, $300B+ FY27 rev
**Base (50%):** $200-240 — Growth moderates to 40-50%, margins stabilize ~72-74%
**Bear (25%):** $120-160 — Capex cycle peaks, ASICs gain share, margins <70%, multiple compresses

## Rating: HOLD — Fair Value ~$210-220

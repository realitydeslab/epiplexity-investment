# GOOGL Learnings & Intelligence

*Research Date: March 6, 2026*

## What This Tells Us About the Sector

1. **Hyperscaler capex cycle is real and accelerating.** Google's $175-185B capex for 2026 (>2x 2025) confirms that AI infrastructure demand is not slowing. MSFT, AMZN deploying similar budgets. This is bullish for NVDA, AVGO, MRVL, VRT, and the entire AI infrastructure chain.

2. **Cloud is the AI delivery mechanism.** Google Cloud's 48% growth and $240B backlog prove enterprises are consuming AI through cloud. The "AI winter" thesis is wrong for 2026.

3. **AI cost deflation is dramatic.** 78% reduction in Gemini serving costs in one year. This means AI will become accessible to smaller businesses faster than expected — expanding the total addressable market.

4. **Big Tech is entering a "capex supercycle."** FCF margins compressing across all hyperscalers as they invest. Short-term pain for long-term moat. Patient investors get rewarded; momentum traders get frustrated.

## Contrarian Angle — What's the Market Missing?

1. **Waymo is worth $30-50B+** and it's valued at zero in most GOOGL models (buried in "Other Bets" losses). At $16B latest valuation and scaling to 6+ cities, a spin-off or IPO could unlock massive value.

2. **The antitrust discount is too large.** GOOGL trades at 26x forward vs MSFT at 35x. The 9-turn gap implies the market fears a 25% earnings hit from DOJ remedies — which is extremely unlikely even in the worst case.

3. **YouTube is an undermonetized beast.** At $11.4B/quarter ($45B+ annual), YouTube is still not fully monetizing Shorts, Connected TV, or AI-generated content. This could be a $70B+ revenue business by 2028.

4. **DeepMind's non-search AI applications** — drug discovery (Isomorphic Labs), weather prediction, materials science — could become a meaningful revenue source. Market gives zero credit.

# GOOGL Growth Velocity — Alphabet/Google

*Research Date: March 6, 2026*

## Revenue Growth: ACCELERATING ✅

| Period | Revenue | YoY Growth |
|--------|---------|------------|
| FY 2023 | $307.4B | 8.7% |
| FY 2024 | $350.0B | 13.9% |
| FY 2025 | $402.8B | 15.1% |
| Q4 2025 | $113.8B | 18.0% |
| FY 2026E | $485.6B | 20.5% (consensus) |

**Verdict:** Revenue growth is clearly ACCELERATING — from 8.7% → 13.9% → 15.1% → 18.0% in Q4. Consensus expects 20.5% for FY2026. For a $400B+ revenue company, this is exceptional.

## Google Cloud — The Star ⭐

- Q4 2025: $17.66B (+48% YoY) — CRUSHED estimates of $16.18B
- Backlog: $240B (+55% QoQ, >2x YoY)
- Cloud is now >15% of revenue and growing 3x faster than the company
- Margin expansion: Cloud operating margins have been steadily improving

## Profitability: EXPANDING ✅

| Metric | FY 2023 | FY 2024 | FY 2025 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Gross Margin | 56.6% | 58.2% | 59.7% |
| Operating Margin | 27.4% | 32.1% | 32.0% |
| Net Margin | 24.0% | 28.6% | 32.8% |
| EPS | $5.80 | $8.05 | $10.82 |
| EPS Growth | 27.2% | 38.8% | 34.4% |

**EPS growing faster than revenue** = operating leverage working. Net margin expanded from 24% → 33% in 2 years.

## Guidance vs. Actuals

Alphabet consistently beats estimates:
- Q4 2025: EPS $2.82 vs $2.63 est (beat by 7.2%)
- Q4 2025: Revenue $113.83B vs $111.43B est (beat by 2.2%)
- Q4 2025: Cloud $17.66B vs $16.18B est (beat by 9.1%)

Pattern: Beats on revenue and EPS, guided capex higher than expected.

## User Growth

- Gemini: 750M MAUs (up from 650M QoQ) — 15% QoQ growth
- YouTube: 2B+ logged-in monthly users (dominant)
- Waymo: 15M cumulative trips, expanding markets

## Free Cash Flow

| Year | FCF | Growth |
|------|-----|--------|
| FY 2023 | $69.5B | 15.8% |
| FY 2024 | $72.8B | 4.7% |
| FY 2025 | $73.3B | 0.7% |

⚠️ FCF growth stalling due to massive capex ramp. FCF margin declined from 22.6% → 18.2%. This is the investment phase — watch for FCF recovery in 2027 as capex peaks.

## Growth Velocity Score: 9/10

Revenue accelerating at $400B+ scale is rare. Cloud is a rocket. EPS compounding at 34%+. The only concern is FCF compression from capex, but that's an investment in future growth, not a structural issue.
